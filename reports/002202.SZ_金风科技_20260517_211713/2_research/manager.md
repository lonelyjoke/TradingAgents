**Investment Plan: 002202.SZ (金风科技)**
**Rating: Underweight**
**Date:** 2026-05-17

---

### 1. Rating and Summary

We recommend **Underweight** on 金风科技 (002202.SZ). The company’s operating recovery is real—Q1 2026 revenue +63% YoY, gross margin up 2.6 pp, and the order book remains vast—but the current share price (implied valuation) demands a smooth, durable earnings acceleration that the latest evidence does not support. The bull thesis leans heavily on unverified assumptions about new-order pricing, smooth cash conversion, and a stabilising shareholder base. In contrast, the bear case is anchored in hard, deteriorating signals: cost growth outstripping revenue, a sharp rise in credit impairments, negative Q1 operating cash flow, and a dramatic fragmentation of the shareholder register.  

While 金风科技 is a legitimate global wind leader, the stock today is a poor risk/reward proposition. The probability of near‑term disappointment (Q2 margin stall, further impairment, retail selling) is elevated, and the valuation remains in the top quintile of its five‑year range. Better risk‑adjusted returns exist elsewhere in the wind‑power chain and among peer OEMs. Reducing exposure is the disciplined action.

---

### 2. Core Bet (Bearish)

**We are betting against the market’s expectation of a rapid, high‑quality profit recovery.**  
Specifically, we believe that  
- the gross margin improvement will stall or even reverse as low‑priced legacy orders are still being delivered while new orders are not yet contributing meaningfully to as‑shipped margins;  
- the conversion of revenue into cash will remain strained, forcing the company to fund growth with ever‑larger working‑capital borrowings;  
- credit impairments will continue to rise, eating into an already thin net margin (5.9 % in Q1); and  
- the severe retailisation of the shareholder base will amplify any negative news, turning modest fundamental misses into outsized share‑price declines.

**Expectation gap:**  
The market appears to price a 2026 net profit of roughly ¥35–40 bn (PE TTM 34× on trailing ¥2.77 bn, forward ~29× on annualised Q1). We estimate full‑year net profit closer to ¥30–33 bn, with the quality of that profit being low. The current valuation leaves little room for error; if the recovery stumbles, valuation compression could drive the stock to 18–20 (a 20‑30 % decline), offering a negative expected return.

**Probability/Payoff:**  
We assign roughly 60 % to the bear case (stock trades toward 20‑22 over 6 months) and 40 % to a neutral‑to‑bull case (stable around 24‑28). The expected value is materially lower than current price, justifying an Underweight stance.

---

### 3. Catalyst Path and Falsification

**Downside catalysts (nearest 1–2 quarters):**  
- **2026 half‑year report (August 2026):** if gross margin fails to improve sequentially from Q1’s 16.8 % and credit impairment continues to expand, the market will have to reset earnings assumptions.  
- **Continued reduction by key shareholder Harmony Insurance** and any further retail‑driven volatility could trigger stop‑loss selling.  
- **EU FSR investigation update:** while the company states operations are normal, any formal negative finding would hit the overseas order premium thesis directly.

**Falsification signals (that would upgrade the rating):**  
- Half‑year gross margin ≥ 18 % with positive Q2 operating cash flow.  
- Credit impairments plateau or decline, with receivables stability.  
- Shareholder count stabilises or falls, accompanied by institutional accumulation.  

If those signals materialise, the bear case weakens and we would reconsider. Until then, evidence points to caution.

---

### 4. Cycle‑Valuation Calibration

金风科技 currently sits in a **high‑valuation / low‑prosperity quadrant** when judged on trailing metrics: PE 34× on 2025 earnings, PB 2.67×, both in the 85th–95th percentile of five‑year history. While Q1 showed a cyclical lift, the underlying prosperity (mid‑teens gross margin, single‑digit net margin) is still far below historical peaks.  

For such setups, we normally require a clear, imminent inflection that can digest the valuation. Here, the inflection is only partially visible: gross margin improvement is fragile, and cash conversion has not turned. Therefore, the rating must reflect the risk that the recovery is already priced in, making the stock vulnerable to disappointment.

### 6. Company Quality Verdict

金风科技 is the world’s largest wind turbine manufacturer by annual installations, with a strong domestic market position and a growing global footprint. Its technology capability (20 MW offshore prototype) and integrated wind‑farm development model provide a durable, if cyclical, competitive advantage.  

However, **quality is dampened** by low profitability (mid‑teens gross margin), high asset intensity, and significant balance‑sheet leverage (debt‑to‑assets 71 %). The business model requires continuous heavy capex and working‑capital funding, which strains free cash flow. The company is a capable cyclicals player, not a high‑quality compounder.

---

### 7. Current Odds Verdict

The current odds are **unfavourable**. At roughly ¥25 per share, the market is paying 29 × forward earnings on a recovery that has yet to prove its durability. The most dependable indicators—gross margin trend, credit impairment, and OCF—send mixed warnings. The risk of a downward revision in earnings expectations is high, while the upside from scenario‑type catalysts is already partially factored in.  

We see the probability‑weighted return as negative over the next 6–12 months. Although a catastrophic collapse is not our base case, the skew is sufficiently poor to warrant an Underweight. Do not confuse “the company will survive” with “the stock will outperform.”

---

### 8. Relative Allocation Verdict

Within the **wind‑power supply chain**, the Turbine OEM segment (median v4_score 49.8) currently looks less attractive than Operators (50.8), which offer more stable cash flows and simpler asset valuations. Among OEMs, **三一重能 (688349.SH)** scores higher quality (v4_score 76.6 vs. 62.4) while trading at a lower P/E (25.1 × vs. 34 ×) and lower P/B (1.89 × vs. 2.67 ×), with better growth momentum (net profit +194 % YoY) and a cleaner balance sheet.  

金风科技’s “optionality” in green methanol and investee IPOs does not, in our view, justify the valuation gap relative to the cleaner, more profitable peer. Therefore, **capital is better allocated to higher‑quality names in the same chain** rather than to 金风科技 at current levels.

---

### 9. Management & Capital Allocation Verdict

Management has demonstrated a repeatable ability to seed and exit minority investments (金力永磁, 上纬新材), generating meaningful fair‑value gains. This “investing capability” is a genuine positive. However, the core capital allocation story is mixed:  
- Heavy reinvestment (construction in progress ¥11.5 bn, FY2025 capex ~¥7.5 bn) is tied to wind‑farm ownership and green methanol projects, which are long‑dated and capital‑hungry.  
- The balance sheet is highly leveraged, and free cash flow has been persistently weak after capex.  
- Share buybacks are a supportive signal, but the scale is modest relative to the equity dilution from retained earnings erosion.  

Overall, management is competent but not exceptional in creating shareholder value; the capital‑allocation tilt toward asset‑heavy growth keeps returns compressed.

---

### 10. Shareholder Structure Verdict

This is **a major red flag**.  
- Harmony Insurance has slashed its stake from 11.2 % (Jun 2025) to 2.7 % (Mar 2026) – a forced/strategic exit that removes a formerly stable holder.  
- The number of registered shareholders has exploded from ~200 k to **692 k** in the same period, indicating massive retail accumulation.  
- While northbound (HK Connect) buying to 4.5 % and some social security fund entry provide a partial offset, the **structural shift from committed institutional money to fragmented retail** creates a dangerously unstable ownership base. Any negative news could trigger a cascading sell‑off that fundamentals alone may not halt.  

This single factor significantly raises the downside risk profile and reinforces the Underweight call.

---

### 11. Key Evidence Review (Debate Synthesis)

**Bull’s strongest arguments:**  
- Q1 gross margin improvement is real, large‑scale turbine mix and overseas orders are lifting margins.  
- Contract liabilities (prepaid orders) provide revenue visibility.  
- The 2026‑E3 market‑wide bid price of ¥1,609 /KW suggests the pricing downturn is ending.  
- Core earnings (ex‑non‑recurring) are now driving profit.

**Bear’s strongest counterarguments (supported by data):**  
- Q1 cost growth (+73.9 %) still outpaces revenue growth (+63.5 %), implying unit revenue may still be declining when adjusted for volume (+133 % units sold). This casts doubt on the “pricing recovery” narrative.  
- Credit impairment surged +70 % to ¥57 M, signalling that rapid volume growth is coming with deteriorating receivable quality, not just seasonal lumpiness.  
- Q1 operating cash flow was –¥1.55 bn, and while seasonal, the magnitude relative to net profit underscores the cash‑intensive nature of the current expansion.  
- Shareholder base melt‑down is a structural headwind, not a footnote.  
- Blue Arrow stake is immaterial (<1 % of market cap) and green methanol is years from revenue contribution.

The bear’s case is **better anchored in recent, verified, and deteriorating metrics**, while the bull’s case relies on forward‑looking assumptions that lack confirmation. We weigh the bear evidence more heavily.

---

### 12. Actionable Plan

- **Reduce existing positions** to underweight versus the benchmark or peer group.  
- **Set a strict watch level:** re‑evaluate if the half‑year report shows gross margin ≥ 18 %, positive Q2 OCF, and stable‑to‑declining credit impairments.  
- **Avoid adding new exposure** at current levels. If the stock retreats to the 18–20 range on fundamental disappointment, a reassessment toward “Hold” may be warranted, but that would require evidence of stabilisation in the above metrics.  
- **Prefer** operators (e.g., 中闽能源) or the higher‑quality OEM 三一重能 for wind‑chain exposure.

---

**Conclusion:** The bull story is plausible medium‑term but not investable at today’s valuation given the immediate evidence of earnings‑quality stress and shareholder‑structure risk. Until the next catalyst proves the recovery is clean and the ownership base stabilises, the prudent path is to underweight 002202.SZ.
