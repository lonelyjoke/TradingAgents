### 4. Expectation Gap: Market Has Priced a "Permanent Earnings Lift" – We See Only Cyclical Peak Earnings

The bull’s valuation model (e.g., 40x PE even in a base case of RMB 4.5bn profit) assumes the market will not apply a cyclical discount. Historical precedent for standardized chip cycles (e.g., DRAM/NOR cycles) shows that at the peak, multiples compress rapidly to 10–20x once earnings roll over. The current 88x TTM PE (on what is likely peak earnings) is extreme.  

**Our neutral probability-weighted scenario (based on analyst debate):**  
- 20% chance of sustained high earnings (RMB 5.5–6.0 bn) → fair value ~RMB 236–300 (using 30–35x PE, still a cycle-premium multiple).  
- 50% chance of moderate rollover (RMB 4.0–4.5 bn) → fair value ~RMB 103–141 (18–22x PE).  
- 30% chance of sharp collapse (RMB 3.0–3.5 bn) → fair value ~RMB 51–75 (12–15x PE).  

**Expected value: ~RMB 120–180, implying >50% downside from RMB 362.** The bull’s 9.1% expected return (from their own model) assumes the market will never apply cycle-adjusted multiples – a heroic assumption.
