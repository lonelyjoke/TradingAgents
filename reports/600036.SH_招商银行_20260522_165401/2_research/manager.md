### Company Quality Verdict
China Merchants Bank (`600036.SH`) remains one of the highest-quality joint-stock banks in China, with a durable competitive moat built on a low-cost retail deposit franchise, a leading wealth-management platform (“金葵花” and “一卡通” ecosystem), and above‑average technology investment (2025 R&D spend = 4.31% of revenue). Its ROE has historically held above 12%, and the parent company has been consistently profitable with net profit ~150.2 bn in 2025. The business model is shifting toward “asset‑light” fee‑based income, which provides some structural resilience against net‑interest‑margin (NIM) compression. However, the bank is not immune to the industry‑wide headwinds of slowing loan demand and declining margins. The core retail banking engine saw revenue growth decelerate to roughly 1% in 2025, and the 2026 outlook is uncertain. On balance, the franchise is strong but currently navigating a low‑growth cyclical trough.

Over the last 6 months, the EPS proxy rose 1.2% yet PE contracted another 14.2%, pushing the price down 13.2%. This pattern confirms a valuation‑led derating while earnings held up.
- **Expectation gap:** The market appears to be discounting modest earnings erosion; our core bet is that the actual outcome will be stagnation or slight recovery rather than a structural decline, and that this will lead to a partial re‑rating of the multiple toward the historical median (7–8× PE). Even if earnings stay flat, a move to 7.0× PE would imply ~¥42 (13% upside plus a ~5% dividend yield).  
- **Downside protection:** The 0.82× PB and 6.19× PE already sit near cyclical lows, limiting absolute downside unless the credit cycle turns significantly worse or NIM collapses further—scenarios not yet evidenced in the available data.

**Conviction is capped by the lack of verified Q1 2026 segment‑level earnings detail** (see Evidence Gaps below). The probability of the bull scenario is estimated at roughly 55–60% over a 12‑month horizon, producing a positive expected value.
