## Core Bet

The market has mispriced a **cyclical destocking event** as a **structural decline in brand power**. The 62% sequential collapse in contract liabilities (from 8.0bn in FY2025 to 3.0bn in Q1 2026) is being interpreted by the market as permanent demand destruction, but the bull thesis argues it is a deliberate active-destocking measure to clean channel inventory, consistent with the simultaneous surge in operating cash flow (+205% YoY) and revenue recovery (+6.54% YoY). The gross margin compression of 1.42pp is a transitory mix shift toward higher series-liquor sales (which grew +11.25% in FY2025 volume), not a structural margin erosion. The 1,800 tonnes of additional Moutai premium capacity (commissioned Oct 2025, output releasing in 2026) will lift both top-line growth and margin in the second half of 2026.

## Fair Cycle-Valuation Calibration

**Setup: Low Valuation / Low Prosperity**

The company is experiencing a cyclical trough (revenue decline of -1.21% in FY2025, profit flat in Q1 2026) while the PE multiple is at a 5-year low. This is the classic cyclical trough setup. The key question is whether the downturn is structural or cyclical.

- **Evidence for cyclical:** Operating cash flow is recovering, revenue growth has turned positive, and the industry macro data (tobacco & alcohol retail sales +15.2% YoY Jan-Apr 2026) suggests end-demand is stabilizing. The volume contraction in the industry is real, but Moutai is taking share.
- **Evidence for structural:** Contract liabilities collapsed, gross margin is contracting, and the Baijiu industry has shrunk for nine consecutive years. The bear case argues that Moutai’s growth model is permanently impaired.

**Verdict:** The bull case (cyclical) is more consistent with the filing evidence because the cash-flow data and revenue inflection would not occur in a structural decline. Pessimism appears fully priced at 19.9x PE. The downside is cushioned by the buyback, dividend, and net cash.
