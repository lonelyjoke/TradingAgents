3. **Overseas‑expansion (business‑realisation)** – Disclosed foreign asset revenue scale confirms geographic diversification. It enters core valuation only to the extent that it broadens the earnings runway; the current rating does not rely on a separate overseas premium.

All three themes are “filing‑backed + monetisation evidence”; none is mere concept. No unverified theme is used to raise the rating.

The **15 bn USD zero‑coupon convertible** (January 2026) is a financing event. It does not harm the operating thesis but will create future dilution if converted above the implied premium. We treat it as a known supply‑overhang for extreme upside scenarios, not a near‑term headwind.

**Company Quality Verdict**
Zijin is a **rare, high‑return mining compounder**. Its 31.8 % ROE, 18.1 % ROA, >60 % mine‑level gross margin, and 1.46× cash conversion place it in the top tier of global copper‑gold producers. The asset base is diversified across geography and commodity, with a demonstrated ability to execute both greenfield development (Kamoa) and opportunistic M&A (recent move for SGE‑listed gold name). Balance‑sheet leverage (51.4 % debt/asset) is manageable given cash‑flow generation, though it limits the margin of safety in a deep price crash. The company has consistently grown production and reserves, and its cost position on the cost curve is structurally advantaged. **Overall, business quality is well above average** for the sector.

***Safety Price / Defensive Build Anchor***
For a cyclical miner, a traditional safety price is problematic because the underlying commodity is the primary risk. However, using a conservative normalised-earnings framework:
- Assume through‑cycle copper at ¥95,000/t and gold at ¥900/g; volume held at 2026E run‑rate; consolidated gross margin reverts to 28–30 %, net profit ~CNY 45‑50 bn.  
- At a fair PE of 15× (mid‑cycle), implied market cap ≈ CNY 700‑750 bn, translating to **~CNY 26‑28 per share**.  
This band provides a rough accumulation floor; below ¥26, the stock would be pricing in a genuine commodity bust, which we view as unlikely given the structural copper deficit. **A break below ¥25 on a verified collapse in copper fundamentals or a sustained gold bear would invalidate the safety floor.** Consequently, aggressive building in the ¥29‑30 zone is acceptable, but tight stop‑losses should be placed below ¥25 on confirmed fundamental deterioration, not on intraday volatility.
