### Core Bet & Expectation Gap

**Core bet:** The market has priced aluminium supply tightness as if it were durable, but the profit surge is largely cyclical (supply shock + unverified demand). Insider selling and technical deterioration signal that current valuation (12.5x PE, 35.6% ROE) is a **low-valuation/high-prosperity trap** – cheap only if extraordinary earnings persist.

**Expectation gap:** The bull thesis assumes a structural shift (deep processing, cost advantage) can sustain high margins. Evidence shows >90% of profit still depends on aluminium ingot price. The “structural” story lacks scale; demand-side data (construction, auto, power) is absent. The market may already be discounting mean reversion – a 12.5x PE for a peak-cycle commodity producer is not cheap; it is fair risk pricing.

### Key Evidence (from research plan & debate)

- **Supply tightness (verified):** LME inventory declining, spot premiums at 2007 highs, Chinese aluminium exports at record – all confirm supply-side stress.
- **Insider selling (high weight):** Major shareholder announced planned reduction at peak profitability. This is an **already-factored negative signal** of internal conviction.
- **Technical (high weight):** Medium-term breakdown below 31.12 – market has already voted with capital.
- **Financial (high weight):** 35.6% ROE, gross margin doubling, 223% net profit jump – textbook cyclical peak.

**Missing evidence (critical gap):**  
No micro-level demand data (construction starts, auto output, power grid orders). No Q2 2026 operational data to verify whether margins can withstand a flat/falling aluminium price. No product spreads (e.g., alumina vs. aluminium) to assess cost pass-through.  
**These gaps cap conviction and label the bear view as evidence-limited.** We rely on macro proxies (PMI) and insider/trade data, not micro fundamentals.
