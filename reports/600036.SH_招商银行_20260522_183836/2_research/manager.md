**Rating: Overweight**

**Core Bet**
The market is pricing a structural earnings crisis. The verifiable evidence—EPS at an all-time high, Q1 2026 revenue accelerating to +3.8% YoY, and fee income stabilizing—contradicts this narrative. Our bet is that the negative earnings-revision cycle is ending. As this becomes clear in Q2/Q3 reports, the valuation multiple will re-rate from a crisis-level 6.2x PE toward a more normal 7-8x range, unlocking significant total return potential.

**Expectation Gap**
The Price-EPS-PE decomposition is decisive: over 5 years, EPS rose 48.4% while PE compressed 53.9%. The stock’s decline is purely a valuation-led derating, not an earnings-led collapse. The market-equity context shows that current earnings are already sufficient to justify the TTM PE, meaning the market is not pricing in growth at all. It is pricing in permanent stagnation or decline. The Q1 2026 data (single-quarter revenue growth at a 3-year high, fee income +4.4% YoY) shows this is too pessimistic.

**Catalyst Path**
The primary catalyst is the release of the 2026 semi-annual report (expected August/September 2026). Confirmation of Q1’s revenue momentum, stabilization or QoQ improvement in NIM, and continued fee income recovery will force the market to recalibrate its growth expectations. A secondary catalyst is any official signal from the PBOC regarding a pause in LPR cuts, which would immediately anchor NIM expectations.

**Falsification Signals**
The Overweight thesis would be invalidated if the Q2 2026 report shows: (1) revenue growth decelerating below 2% YoY, (2) NIM declining another 5-10bp sequentially to ~1.3% without a clear near-term floor, or (3) a sharp deterioration in retail loan asset quality (the Bear’s key risk).

**Conviction Level**
Medium-High. The asymmetry is strong, but a final confirmation of the NIM bottom is missing. We will use a staged entry plan. Build an initial position now based on the expectation gap; increase to full weight when the semi-annual report confirms the stabilization thesis.
