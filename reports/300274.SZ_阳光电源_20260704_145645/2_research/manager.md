# Research Manager Accepted Underwriting Model

**Rating**: Hold

**Research Readiness**: partial

**Core Bet**: That the record contract liabilities (122 bn) and aggressive prepaid material stocking (prepayments +201% QoQ) will convert into revenue acceleration and cash recovery in H2 2026, proving that Q1's profit slump was a timing issue rather than a demand or margin collapse.

### 本报告要回答的关键问题

1. Can energy storage system gross margin be sustained near 30% in 2026-2027 despite rising competition and raw material volatility?
2. How will US tariffs (currently 38.4%) affect Sungrow's US energy storage orders and profitability over 2026?
3. Will the massive contract liabilities and prepayments actually translate into accelerated H2 revenue and improved operating cash flow?
4. Is the EU policy restriction on Chinese inverters for public-funded projects a manageable risk or a material threat to European revenue and margin?

### 核心问题裁决

**Q1：Can energy storage system gross margin be sustained near 30% in 2026-2027?**
- **为何关键：** Energy storage is the second growth curve; its margin trajectory directly determines consolidated profit growth and whether 20x+ PE can be justified.
- **当前结论：** Q1 2026 energy storage gross margin was ~30%, driven by a favourable regional mix shift towards high-margin Europe. However, the sustainability of this mix is uncertain because the EU is moving to restrict Chinese inverters, and the improvement is measured QoQ (not YoY). Margin resilience is real but fragile.
- **采用证据：** Investor interaction (2026-05-14): Q1 energy storage gross margin ~30%, regional mix improvement.；Filing: Q1 2026 consolidated gross margin 33.26% (-1.87pp YoY) indicates overall margin pressure despite segment resilience.；Knowledge Planet KPE01-KPE04: channel checks mention positive order tone, but lack specific margin quantification.；Policy context: EU guidance to exclude high-risk country inverters from public funds (2025).
- **最强反证：** The EU policy shift directly targets the high-margin European public-fund market, which is likely the source of the regional mix improvement. If that market contracts, the 30% margin may not be repeatable.
- **模型/估值影响：** We keep the base case assumption of energy storage gross margin ~30% for 2026E, but apply a higher discount rate in the scenario valuation to reflect policy risk. Upside scenario still allows 33% margin if European non-public markets grow, but probability reduced to 30%.
- **置信度与下次验证：** medium；2026 half-year report: energy storage revenue and gross margin by region; any update on EU policy enforcement and its impact on order book.

**Q2：How will US tariffs (currently 38.4%) affect US energy storage orders and profitability?**
- **为何关键：** The US is a high-margin market for energy storage, and the 38.4% tariff can devastate the profit pool if not mitigated by supply-chain shifts or customer absorption.
- **当前结论：** The tariff is a serious near-term headwind. 2025 saw a pull-forward of orders under the safe-harbor window, leaving 2026 exposed. New orders exist but are likely much smaller. The bull case of sustained growth is not supported; the bear case that the US market is severely impaired is more plausible, but not yet a full shut-down.
- **采用证据：** Investor interaction (2026-05-13): US tariff 38.4%, 2026 new orders exist, but 2025 pre-orders depleted.；Q1 2026 revenue decline -18% YoY: consistent with US order drain.；Competitor analysis: none of the peers have offsetting US advantage.
- **最强反证：** Sungrow may transfer supply chain to Thailand/other regions to reduce tariff exposure, and non-US markets (Europe, Middle East) can partially compensate. However, no public announcement of a supply-chain relocation has been made.
- **模型/估值影响：** We reduce 2026E US energy storage revenue by 40% and apply a lower gross margin (base case 25% vs 30% company-wide). This reduces 2026E consolidated net profit by ~5% relative to the original underwriting model.
- **置信度与下次验证：** medium；Half-year report disclosure on overseas energy storage revenue and any announcement of supply-chain localisation.

**Q3：Will the massive contract liabilities and prepayments actually translate into accelerated H2 revenue and improved operating cash flow?**
- **为何关键：** This is the core bet. If conversion fails, the stock is a value trap with ballooned working capital; if it succeeds, a sharp earnings recovery will re-rate the stock.
- **当前结论：** The combination of contract liabilities (+14.5% QoQ) and prepaid materials (+201% QoQ) strongly points to an upcoming delivery surge. However, Q1 operating cash flow (OCF/NI = 0.53) and the rise in inventory/AR indicate that cash conversion is poor even before the surge. Without a concurrent improvement in receivables and inventory turnover, accelerated revenue could further strain cash flow rather than restore it. The bull case has a plausible order view, but the cash flow deterioration undermines its certainty.
- **采用证据：** Q1 2026 filings: contract liabilities 122.04 bn, prepayments 40.39 bn, OCF/NI 0.53, receivables/revenue 40%, inventory/revenue 44.6%.；Investor interaction: prepayments are 'mainly to lock in raw material supply', confirming active stocking.；Channel checks (KPE01-04): indicate positive scheduling and backlog, but are private/unverified.
- **最强反证：** The sharp OCF deterioration and bloated working capital (inventory+AR >50% of annual revenue) may signal that customers are delaying payment and that the order backlog is not being converted to cash. This is a structural warning that the bull thesis must address before the rating can be upgraded.
- **模型/估值影响：** We maintain the base case revenue of 945 bn for 2026E, but we lower the OCF/Net income assumption from 0.8 to 0.7 due to working capital drag. This reduces FCF and the present value of equity. The probability of the bull case (H2 surge with cash recovery) is capped at 20% until we see improvement in cash metrics.
- **置信度与下次验证：** low；2026 half-year report: Q2 revenue, OCF, AR and inventory turnover, and contract liability movement. The bull case requires Q2 revenue +20% QoQ and OCF/NI >0.7.

**Q4：Is the EU policy restriction on Chinese inverters a manageable risk or a material threat?**
- **为何关键：** Europe is Sungrow's highest-margin overseas region. If the restriction expands beyond public funds or blocks all public projects, a significant profit pool could be lost.
- **当前结论：** The policy currently targets only EU public-funded projects, limiting the immediate financial impact. Sungrow is proactively building local supply chains and expanding non-public-fund business. However, the direction of policy is clearly towards de-risking from Chinese hardware, and there is a material risk of escalation. It is a manageable risk in the base case, but a tail risk for the bear case.
- **采用证据：** Investor interaction (2026-05-13): company assesses 'limited impact' and is expanding non-official-financing projects and localising supply.；Policy context: EU Commission guidance restricting 'high-risk country' inverters.；Filing: no specific disclosure on European revenue exposure.
- **最强反证：** The policy is an 'administrative guideline', not a binding regulation, but it already forces Sungrow to spend on localisation and diversify away from the most profitable European segment, potentially eroding gross margin.
- **模型/估值影响：** We apply a 10% haircut to European revenue in the bear scenario and a 20% haircut to the segment's gross margin, but keep the base case unchanged with a watch. This increases the bear-case downside to 80 bn net profit.
- **置信度与下次验证：** medium；Any EU official clarification, Sungrow's announcement of a European factory, or disclosure of European revenue share in the half-year report.

## Ranked Core Theses

### 论点1：The order backlog and prepaid materials point to a near-term revenue recovery, but the conversion to cash is highly uncertain, making the investment thesis conditionally viable at a lower price.

- **关键问题：** Will contract liabilities translate into H2 revenue acceleration and cash flow recovery?
- **核心证据：** Q1 2026 contract liabilities 122.04 bn (+14.5% QoQ), prepayments 40.39 bn (+201% QoQ); company confirmed prepayments for raw material lock-in (investor interaction).
- **最强反证：** Q1 OCF only 53% of net profit, inventory and AR at multi-year highs relative to sales, indicating that the build-up is consuming cash rather than generating it.
- **财务传导：** Revenue = f(order backlog, delivery schedule); Cash flow = Net profit ± ΔWC; In the base case, 2026E revenue 945 bn (+6%), but FCF is only 6.25 bn (vs 16.9 bn in 2025).
- **市场定价：** Current PE 21.9x implies ~119 bn net profit, suggesting the market is pricing a recovery already. For the stock to outperform, H2 must deliver more than the base case.
- **证伪条件：** If 2026H1 revenue <450 bn (implying <5% YoY growth) and OCF/NI remains below 0.6, the thesis of a cash-producing recovery is falsified.
- **当前裁决：** partial

### 论点2：Sungrow's brand and scale provide some margin insulation, but the industry's competitive dynamics and policy shifts are eroding the moat, limiting the bull case's upside.

- **关键问题：** Can Sungrow maintain a superior gross margin above 30% in the face of price wars and EU de-risking?
- **核心证据：** FY2025 group gross margin 31.83% (+1.89pp), Q1 2026 33.26% (-1.87pp YoY); energy storage margin ~30% QoQ improvement (investor interaction).
- **最强反证：** Q1 overall margin decline YoY and the EU inverter restriction directly threaten the highest-margin region. Competitor margins are also under pressure, signalling industry-wide compression.
- **财务传导：** A 1pp decline in group gross margin reduces pre-tax profit by ~9.5 bn. At current leverage and mix, even a 31% margin would reduce EPS by ~0.3 yuan vs 32%.
- **市场定价：** The current multiple of 21.9x PE reflects some premium for historical margin superiority, but does not fully price a margin decline to 29-30%.
- **证伪条件：** If 2026H1 gross margin falls below 30%, the margin-moat narrative is broken and the stock would be re-rated to below 15x PE.
- **当前裁决：** partial

### 论点3：The best expression in the electrical equipment sector is not Sungrow at current levels; higher-quality peers offer better risk-adjusted returns, making Sungrow a relative underweight until the cash cycle improves.

## Company Disaggregation

Sungrow (300274.SZ) is a clean energy equipment manufacturer with three main economic units: (1) PV Inverters – core profit engine, likely ~55-60% of revenue, driven by global solar installations, ASP, and market share; gross margin historically >31%. (2) Energy Storage Systems – second growth curve, ~30-35% of revenue, driven by order backlog, regional mix, and battery costs; Q1 2026 margin ~30%. (3) Hydrogen Electrolyzers – early-stage option, <5% of revenue, currently loss-making. Additionally, the company holds a significant portfolio of clean energy investment assets (210 bn, ~18.6% of total assets) that provide a NAV floor. Segment-level revenue/profit splits are not disclosed by management, so all weights are analytical estimates.

## Thesis-to-Financial Bridge

The investment case rests on three bridges:

1. **Order Backlog to Revenue Bridge (TB1):** Operating driver: Contract liabilities → delivery schedule → revenue recognition. Base assumption: 2026E revenue 945 bn, requiring ~75% of the 122 bn contract liabilities to convert into revenue in H2. Bull case: 90% conversion, revenue 990 bn. Bear case: 60% conversion, revenue 890 bn. *Impact:* 10% revenue swing = ~1.2 bn net profit.

2. **Margin Resilience Bridge (TB2):** Group gross margin = f(product mix, ASP, input costs). Base: 31.3% (down 0.5pp). Bull: 33% if European high-margin mix holds and inverter ASP decline is contained. Bear: 29% if EU policy forces relocation costs and price war intensifies. *Impact:* 1pp gross margin = ~0.32 CNY EPS.

3. **Cash Conversion Bridge (TB3):** OCF = Net profit ± ΔWorking Capital. Base: OCF/NI 0.7, implying 8.05 bn cash generation. Bear: 0.5, implying 5.75 bn. *Impact:* A sustained low conversion ratio will force debt increase and constrain dividend/buyback capacity.

## Moat Evidence Verdict

Sungrow's claimed moats are partially verified:
- **Brand/R&D premium:** Supported by 5-year R&D investment >5% of revenue and gross margin above industry (FY2025 31.83%). However, Q1 2026 YoY margin decline (-1.87pp) raises questions about the durability of the premium. Counterevidence: EU policy and US tariffs may force price concessions. Status: **partial**.
- **Global scale and localisation:** Overseas revenue likely >50%, and the company is building local supply chains. But EU restriction is a direct threat. Status: **partial**.
- **Full-solution cross-sell:** Inverter+storage+hydrogen bundling is a narrative, but no quantified cross-sell ratio is disclosed. Status: **unproven**.

Overall, the moat is intact but eroding, justifying a mid-cycle PE rather than a premium.

## Debate Verdict

The bull and bear targets highlighted the central tension: the order book is strong, pointing to near-term revenue acceleration, but the cash cycle is deteriorating, which casts doubt on the quality of any recovery. The bull correctly identified the contract liabilities + prepayments signal as a clear indicator of upcoming deliveries. The bear successfully attacked the thesis by focusing on the Q1 cash conversion breakdown and the policy risks in both the US and Europe.

On balance, the bear's case has more near-term evidence support (OCF decline, elevated working capital, unproven EU policy risk), while the bull's case remains a plausible but unverified scenario that requires H1 financials to validate. Consequently, we accept only minor revisions: we keep the base revenue forecast intact (945 bn) but reduce the OCF assumption and factor in higher financing costs, tilting the model towards the lower end of the range. The bull's aggressive upgrades are not accepted pending hard data.

The debate did not resolve the core question; it merely highlighted that the risk/reward is unattractive at the current price because the market already prices a substantial recovery. The recommendation should remain Hold with a negative bias.
