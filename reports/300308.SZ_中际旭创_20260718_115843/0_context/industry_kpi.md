- Customer / End Market: verify top-customer concentration, handset/auto/AI/server/industrial demand, channel inventory and sell-through; explain impact on cycle stage and demand risk.
- Foundry / Packaging Cost: verify wafer foundry allocation, node cost, packaging/test cost, yield, NRE and mask cost; explain impact on gross-margin bridge and supply constraint.
- Inventory / Orders: verify inventory days, distributor inventory, backlog, contract liabilities/prepayments and cancellation risk; explain impact on turning point and revenue quality.
- R&D / IP Moat: verify R&D intensity, IP blocks, software ecosystem, architecture stickiness and replacement risk; explain impact on moat durability and multiple support.
- Valuation / Optionality: verify core EPS/FCF, product-cycle SOTP, verified AI/auto/server optionality and market-implied revenue/profit gap; explain impact on fair value, probability and double-counting control.

## Analyst Instructions
- Do not let a generic sector label replace the KPI map. State which KPI layers are verified, partial, or missing.
- If the report uses cycle language, connect it to at least demand, price/spread, inventory/backlog, and capacity/utilization evidence.
- For semiconductor companies, do not use consumer-style PE shortcuts. Split core value, cycle value, and verified product/technology optionality; triangulate PE with EV/EBITDA, DCF/FCF, ROIC/PB or SOTP as appropriate.
- If a thesis-critical KPI is missing, move it to the retrieval/verification calendar as neutral non-evidence instead of converting it into a hard trigger or rating adjustment.
