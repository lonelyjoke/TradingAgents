## Trading Recommendation: 002594.SZ (BYD)

**Action: SELL (Reduce exposure)**  
**Conviction: Moderate** (evidence gaps weaken both sides, but the risk/reward asymmetry is unfavorable)

---

### Core Bet & Expectation Gap

The bull thesis bets on a H2 2026 profit recovery driven by the end of the price war and overseas volume growth, with contract liabilities rising 5.7% QoQ as a leading indicator.  
The bear thesis bets that Q1's weak results (annualized net profit ~16.3bn CNY vs FY2025's 32.6bn) are not a trough but a structural over-capacity problem, with cash flow erosion and a premium PE (30.9x TTM) unsupported by fundamentals.

**Our assessment:** The market is pricing a “normal” recovery to ~27.5bn CNY TTM earnings, which still leaves the stock at 30.9x – far above its own 5-year median PE of 22.6x and peer median 14-17x. The bull case requires earnings **above** FY2025 level to justify the current multiple, yet no verified evidence (overseas margins, cost trends, or order-to-revenue conversion) supports such a rebound. The bear case has plausible but unverified cost assumptions (lithium prices, chip costs), but the valuation itself provides a margin of safety only if earnings recover. In a high-valuation/low-prosperity setup, the downside from multiple compression is the dominant risk.

### Re-Entry Watch Range (for new buyers or covered shorts)

- **Valuation trigger:** PE falling to **20-22x** on a mid-cycle earnings base (e.g., 28-32bn CNY). At current earnings, this implies a price ~28% below spot.
- **Earnings evidence trigger:** Q2 2026 must show:
  - Operating cash flow positive (vs Q1 negative)
  - Contract liabilities >55bn CNY (sustain demand visibility)
  - Gross margin stabilization or improvement (currently unverified due to lack of product-level data)
- **Cost verification trigger:** Confirmation that lithium carbonate and chip costs are not rising further (currently an evidence gap – see below).

---

### Critical Research Gaps That Cap Conviction

1. **No product-level price/margin data** – The bull case hinges on the end of the price war, but no filing or industry report verifies that BYD's realized ASP has stopped falling or that overseas margins are improving.
2. **No commodity cost mapping** – Claims of lithium and chip cost surges (+125% and +180%) are unverified. If true, margin recovery is impossible; if false, the bear case is overstated. This is the single most important missing piece.
3. **No overseas revenue/profit split** – The overseas expansion theme (1mn units exported) is monetization-unverified; we do not know whether overseas operations are profitable or cash-draining.
4. **No capacity utilization or sell-through data** – The "capacity-release" theme remains scenario upside until utilization rates and inventory turns are disclosed.

---

### Falsification Signals for the Bear Thesis (i.e., what would change our mind)

- **Bullish upgrade triggers:**
  - Q2 2026 revenue >200bn and net profit >8bn (run-rate above 32bn)
  - Operating cash flow >15bn in Q2
  - Contract liabilities >60bn
  - Any signed long-term contract with a major overseas fleet/rental operator
- **Bearish confirmation (stay SELL):**
  - Q2 net profit below 5bn
  - Contract liabilities declining again
  - Short-term borrowings continuing to rise while cash from operations stays negative

---

### Summary for Trader

BYD is a high-quality franchise in a valuation trap. The current price requires earnings to recover to 2025 levels and then some – an assumption not yet supported by filing or industry data. Reduce now with staged exits, and only consider re-entering on clear earnings proof and favorable valuation. Do not treat recent price weakness as a bargain; the 0.3-percentile PB reflects capital that has not yet generated returns.
