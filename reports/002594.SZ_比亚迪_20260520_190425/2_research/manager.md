### Unchanged Core Facts
- BYD remains the global leader in new‑energy vehicles (NEV), with vertical integration across batteries, semiconductors, and vehicle assembly.
- 2025 full‑year revenue was 804 bn CNY, net profit 32.6 bn CNY, and exports exceeded 1 million units.

- The company continues heavy capital expenditure (156.8 bn in FY2025) on overseas capacity and battery technology.

These fundamentals are not in dispute; the question is whether the current price compensates for the risks.

### Core Bet Evaluation

The **bull thesis** bets that BYD is at a cyclical trough: a recent industry‑wide price hike signals the end of a brutal price war, while overseas volume growth and contract‑liability expansion imply profit recovery in H2 2026, making the current 30.9x PE a chance to buy before the inflection.  
The **bear thesis** bets that the Q1 deterioration is not merely a trough but a symptom of over‑capacity, cash‑flow erosion, and a failing capital‑allocation model, and that the stock’s premium valuation will compress as earnings fail to rebound.

After evaluating both sides against the available data, the **bear case carries more weight**, but several critical bear arguments are themselves unverified. The bull case contains too many evidence gaps around pricing power and overseas margins to warrant a constructive rating. The balance of probability/payoff favours a cautious, underweight posture.

**Conclusion on valuation:** The market is not pricing in a disaster; it is pricing in a moderate recovery that still leaves BYD as an expensive stock. To justify the current quote, one needs either much higher earnings (e.g. >35 bn) or a sustained re‑rating above historical multiples – both of which lack sufficient evidentiary support.

### Catalyst Path & Falsification Signals

- **Bull case would strengthen if:** Q2 2026 filings show (a) gross margin recovery to ≥20% and operating margin lift, (b) overseas revenue at least flat or growing, (c) inventory/revenue ratio declining, and (d) contract liabilities holding above 530 bn CNY.
- **Bear case would strengthen to sell territory if:** Q2 shows overseas revenue sharply down, inventory continues to rise faster than contract liabilities, gross margin stagnates, or operating cash flow remains deeply negative.

We recommend a watch‑list of these variables for the next quarterly report.

- **Overseas expansion:** Annual report confirms factory progress, but Q1 data hints at possible revenue decline. Until we see sustained, profitable overseas revenue growth in quarterly filings, this theme qualifies as qualitative optionality, not core valuation.

### Company Quality Verdict
BYD is a genuinely strong business with industry‑leading vertical integration, scale, and technology. Its competitive moat in NEVs and batteries is real. However, the current investment case is about price, not quality.

### Current Odds Verdict
The risk/reward is unfavourable. The stock is priced for a recovery that still leaves it expensive relative to history and peers. The evidence for a margin and earnings inflection is incomplete, while the risks of cash‑flow strain, inventory overhang, and overseas disappointment are tangible. The probability‑adjusted payoff is negative.

### Relative Allocation Verdict
Within the *汽车整车* peer group, BYD ranks poorly on valuation (PE 30.9x vs. peer median <18x), ROE (1.6% vs. decent peers), and cash‑flow quality. Names like 宇通客车 (12.9x PE, ROE 4.1%, lower debt) offer better risk‑adjusted value, albeit with a different growth profile. There is no compelling reason to allocate to BYD over cheaper, higher‑quality peers in the same broad industry.

### Management & Capital Allocation Verdict
Capital allocation is concerning. Capex of 156.8 bn in FY2025, combined with OCF of only 59.1 bn, led to massive negative free cash flow. Short‑term borrowings surged 72% in Q1 2026, while the return on invested capital remains low. The 2025 employee stock ownership plan’s first lock‑up condition was not met, indicating internal performance targets were missed. The heavy investment cycle may eventually pay off, but current capital discipline looks aggressive and is not being rewarded by cash returns.

### Shareholder Structure Verdict
Ownership is concentrated (top‑10 hold 77%), which provides stability. There is no large‑scale insider selling, and a tiny insider buy in 2025 is symbolic at best. Pledge risks are manageable. Retail shareholder count is high (718,724), suggesting a crowded retail base – often a headwind in downturns.

### Investor Communication Verdict
Management responses to investor questions are polite but non‑committal. Repeated questions about rail transit investment and solid‑state batteries receive boilerplate replies. The lack of substantive disclosure on overseas profitability and demand trends is itself a negative signal. The market’s concern map (pricing, technology, strategy) is not being addressed with hard data.

### Policy Direction Verdict
National policy frameworks (“15th Five‑Year Plan,” green transition directive) provide a long‑term tailwind for NEV demand. This supports BYD’s TAM and strategic relevance but does not insulate the company from near‑term competitive and margin pressures. Policy is supportive but not a near‑term catalyst.

### Historical Price‑EPS‑PE Decomposition Verdict
Over 1‑year, the 76% price decline was almost entirely EPS‑driven, while the PE ratio actually expanded slightly. At the current 93.36, the EPS proxy of 3.02 is 28% below the median EPS at similar historical prices, while the PE is 37% higher. Today’s price is therefore more dependent on multiple hope than prior same‑price episodes. This increases the vulnerability should earnings fail to recover.

### Industry Driver Verdict
The core industry driver is the transition from price‑war competition to stable, value‑based pricing. The available evidence (lack of verified price‑hike data, lagging demand indicators) does not yet confirm this shift. Until the industry’s pricing structure demonstrably improves, BYD’s margin risk remains high.

### Commodity Cycle Verdict
No commodity mapping is available for BYD. Key input costs (lithium carbonate, memory chips) are unverified. This is a material research gap; any claims about cost‑push inflation should be treated as assumptions until hard data is obtained.

### Strategic Optionality Verdict
BYD’s optionality in energy storage, semiconductors, and AI datacentre‑relevant technologies is real but not yet monetised to a degree that changes the valuation base case. These remain watch items that could contribute to upside if later filings show material revenue or profit contributions.

2. Overseas revenue disclosure indicating at least flat sequential growth.  
3. Inventory/revenue ratio declining from the current 26.7% toward 20%.  
4. Industry‑wide price stability confirmed in official regulatory or third‑party data (not just narrative).

**Risk scenario for immediate downgrade to Sell:**  
- Q2 overseas revenue down >15% YoY.  
- Contract liabilities falling below 500 bn.  
- Operating cash flow remaining negative while short‑term borrowings rise further.

**Summary for the Trader:**  
BYD is a quality company caught in a valuation trap. The market already expects a recovery that would still leave the stock expensive; the data to justify that recovery is thin. We recommend reducing exposure and watching for the next quarterly checkpoint. Do not treat the 93 CNY level as a bargain – it is a bet on an unproven inflection.
