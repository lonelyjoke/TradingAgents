## Core Bear Bet
**The market is pricing a rapid, high-margin transformation into "AI + environmental services," while actual evidence shows only small, unmonetized proof points and a valuation that already embeds a degree of success the company has not approached.**

The bear expects the gap between narrative and financials to close through derating, not through explosive earnings catch-up. The company's core business generates low, volatile profits; new initiatives (cloud computing, robotics, overseas) are either tiny or cash-burning. The stock's extreme valuation discounts a future that contradicts current data.

### Expectation Gap
- **Market expectation:** Rapid earnings recovery and tech-like re-rating. The 67.9× PE and 98th percentile PB/PS imply investors are underwriting a multi-year compound earnings growth trajectory.
- **Reality:** TTM earnings (~¥583m) barely above 2025 trough. Q1 2026 run-rate (~¥645m) is only 10-17% above last year's depressed level — nowhere near the step-change needed.
- **What would it take?** To normalize at 25× PE and justify ¥39.6bn market cap, earnings must reach ~¥1.58bn. That requires >2.5× current profit — no verified operating metric supports this.

### Probability/Payoff
- **Bull scenario probability:** Low (estimated 15-20%). The transformative drivers (cloud computing, overseas orders, robot ETF inclusion) are either tiny relative to income statement or are costs with no proven revenue.
  - Overseas orders ¥5.4bn = 3.9% of revenue; explosive growth hasn't moved profit needle.
  - Cloud computing depreciation jump to ¥1.56bn (full year 2025) vs segment revenue ¥248m — severe cash burn, not hidden profit pool.
  - No news catalyst confirms monetization.
- **Payoff asymmetry: Deeply unfavorable.**
  - **Upside (optimistic):** If profit recovers to ¥0.8bn and market assigns 25× PE → market cap ¥20bn (49% downside from current ¥39.6bn).
  - **Downside (base):** If transformation narrative fails and stock returns to environmental-sector median 15× PE on trough earnings ¥0.55bn → market cap ¥8.25bn (79% downside). Even 20× PE on flat earnings → ¥11bn (72% downside).
- **Expected value:** Negative. Extreme valuation already discounts a future that actual data contradicts.

### Catalyst Path for Bear Thesis
1. **H1 2026 report (late August):** If cloud computing revenue remains under ¥500m annualized and overseas profit contribution is still invisible, the derating accelerates.
2. **Goodwill impairment:** If any of the ¥5.29bn goodwill is impaired (likely if core margins don't improve), earnings shock hits.
3. **Shareholder dilution / insider selling:** Q&A reveals investor concern about major shareholder (弘创) selling; further supply could pressure price.
4. **Liquidity tightening or municipal budget pressure:** Core sanitation business depends on local government spending; any fiscal headwinds hurt contract renewals and receivables.

### Falsification Signals (what would change our mind)
- **H1 2026 operating profit up >30% YoY** with margin expansion in at least two segments.
- **Cloud computing segment gross profit positive** and revenue exceeding depreciation run-rate (needs >¥1.56bn annualized to break even on depreciation alone).
- **Signed long-term compute leasing contracts** with named customers disclosed in filing.
- **Overseas profit contribution** visible as reported segment profit, not just order volume.

Until one of these materialises, the bear thesis stands.

## Valuation & Cycle Calibration
- **Setup classification:** High valuation / Low prosperity. The stock trades near record multiples while core earnings are depressed and new business is pre-profit.
- **Fair cycle-valuation lens:** Even if we assume a normalization of profits to ¥0.8bn (45% above trough), the current ¥39.6bn market cap implies 49.5× PE — still extreme for an equipment + services company. The sector median is ~15×. A neutral valuation would be ¥0.8bn × 15× = ¥12bn, representing 70% downside from current price.
- **Thematic valuation bridge:** The "AI + environmental services" narrative cannot enter core valuation because:
  - No filing-disclosed revenue, profit, or cash-flow from compute leasing or robotics beyond negligible amounts.
  - Management’s non-committal Q&A answers confirm no material monetization.
  - The cloud computing segment is currently destroying value (depreciation > revenue).
- **Asset revaluation themes:** The long list of investee companies (广东盈峰产业投资, 北京星云智行, etc.) shows no realized investment income or fair-value gains in recent filings. No capital-allocation track record to justify a valuation uplift. The 租赁投资净额 (¥1.37bn) is a financing receivables item, not a catalyst.
- **Conclusion:** At current valuation, the stock is pricing in a transformation that the evidence does not support. Expected return is deeply negative.

## Critical Research Gaps
- **Cloud computing customer contracts / order backlog:** Filings provide revenue and cost but no contract volume, utilization rates, or customer names. This is essential to judge whether the segment is real or merely capital expenditure with no demand.
- **Overseas order profitability:** The ¥5.4bn orders are not broken down by margin or timeline. Without this, it's unknown whether they contribute any profit.
- **Compute leasing specifics:** Investor Q&A requests for details were met with non-committal answers. The lease receivable line item (¥1.37bn) could be related to this, but no segment disclosure confirms. This is the largest unknown: if these leases are high-margin and recurring, the thesis changes. If they are financing equipment sales with thin margins, the thesis weakens further.
- **Public-private partnership (PPP) risk / municipal credit risk:** The company's sanitation contracts are with local governments. No disclosure on aging of receivables or government payment delays.

These gaps cap conviction on the bull case but do not weaken the sell thesis because the burden of proof is on the transformative narrative, and the available evidence contradicts it.
