## PM Summary
**Rating: Underweight (减持)** for `600585.SH` (海螺水泥) as of 2026-06-02. Maintain or trim current position; do not initiate new long.

The debate surfaced a genuine dispute between two rational frameworks, but the final round tilts the table. Bull's core bet is that management will use the 416.6-亿 cash fortress to compress shares and lift per-share intrinsic value faster than earnings decline. Bear's core bet is that the cash-burn rate, when properly measured, is already outrunning the value-creation mechanism, and that management's own capital allocation choices betray a lack of conviction in the buyback thesis.

Bear won the final exchange on the single most dispositive hard fact: **in Q1 2026, management moved 67.6 亿 of cash into 交易性金融资产 (from 128.9 亿 to 196.5 亿) while executing a symbolic 0.023% initial buyback.** That allocation choice—83% of full-year 2025 net profit directed into financial assets rather than into retiring deeply discounted equity—directly contradicts the claim that buyback is "the core driver." If management truly believed 0.55x PB was a no-brainer, they would have tilted the marginal yuan toward repurchase, not toward fund commitments. This single data point materially lowers the probability that the buyback thesis will deliver the expected EPS lift at scale.

Cash-flow momentum also tilts bearish: Q1 2026 operating cash flow of 13.0 亿, down 21.8% YoY, with cash reserves dropping from 70.2 亿 to 41.7 亿 in five quarters. Bull's "2.59x cash/profit" correction is arithmetically correct but economically misleading—it works only because the denominator (recurring net profit) collapsed 69.8% YoY, while the numerator (OCF) is temporarily supported by non-cash depreciation. That ratio is a distress signal, not a fortress signal.

The market at 13.7x PE TTM (66.5% five-year percentile) and 0.55x PB is not obviously mispriced: the PE is elevated *because* earnings are falling, and the PB is low *because* book assets (capacity, goodwill, financial investments) may be impaired in a structural demand decline. No product-price or inventory data exists to confirm a cyclical trough; contract liabilities fell 9.8% QoQ, pointing to further demand deterioration. The bear case has better evidence alignment and the bull case has been materially weakened by management's own actions.

**Action:** Trim or avoid. Wait for either (a) a buyback that actually consumes material cash at scale, or (b) a quarterly filing that shows operating cash flow stabilizing above 40 亿/quarter, before considering a rebuild.

## Expectation Gap
**The market appears to price gradual earnings decline, not a full liquidation scenario.** At 13.7x PE TTM (66.5% five-year percentile), the multiple is elevated for a company with falling earnings. The market is not applying a "liquidation" discount; it is applying a mid-range multiple to declining earnings, implying a bet that earnings will trough and stabilize, not collapse to zero. The bull's claim that 0.55x PB implies "liquidation pricing" conflates book-value discount with earnings-multiple elevation. The two must be read together: the market is saying "book value may be overstated relative to going-concern earnings power, but the earnings stream is not about to disappear."

The largest expectation gap is **around capital allocation**, not cement demand. If management shifted from fund deployment to aggressive buyback at 0.55x PB, the stock could re-rate. The Q1 2026 evidence suggests they are not doing that. Until a future filing shows material buyback scaling or a reversal of the financial-asset build, the expectation gap favors the bear.

## Safety Price / 安全价格区间 / 防御性建仓锚

**No reliable safety price can be assigned with high confidence for 600585.SH.**

*Reasoning:* The company operates in a structurally declining industry (Chinese cement demand) with no visible cyclical trough confirmed by product-price or inventory data. While the balance sheet carries 416.6 亿 of cash and 20.4% leverage, the cash is being actively deployed into financial assets rather than returned to shareholders or held as pure defense. The safety-price methodology for cyclicals requires a trough-earnings estimate anchored in conservative product prices, unit costs, and survivable balance-sheet conditions. Without product-price data and with Q1 2026 recurring net profit at only 5.03 亿 (annualized ~20 亿, vs 2025 full-year net profit of 81.1 亿), trough earnings are highly uncertain. A 10x P/E on trough earnings of 20 亿 implies 3.8 CNY/share; a 12x on trough of 40 亿 implies 9.1 CNY/share. The wide range reflects the absence of reliable trough calibration.

*For investors who must build a position:* The hard asset floor is the net cash position. Total liabilities are approximately 52.3 亿 (2025 year-end) against 货币资金 of 41.7 亿 (Q1 2026). Net cash is approximately -10.6 亿—the company is not in a net cash position on a strict current-liabilities basis, but including 交易性金融资产 of 19.6 亿, liquid assets exceed total borrowings. A slow-build anchor at 0.40-0.45x PB would be approximately 14.6-16.4 CNY/share based on current book value—this is a *distress* level, not a fair-value level, and should only be used by investors with a 3-5 year horizon and conviction in a cement cycle recovery. The conditions that must remain true: no dividend cut, no major impairment charge, operating cash flow remaining positive, and management not further expanding financial-asset allocation at the expense of shareholder returns.
