## Company Quality Verdict
**Business Quality: Above Average, but with Cyclical Fragility**
中国铝业 is a strategically irreplaceable asset: the world's largest integrated aluminum producer with captive bauxite from Guinea, alumina refining, smelting, and downstream processing. The 4,500-tonne smelting capacity ceiling in China creates a structural floor for the industry. Low net debt and high operating cash flow (per bull claims, unverified but plausible) suggest the balance sheet will survive a downturn.

However, the business remains deeply cyclical. Margins are a leveraged bet on aluminum price minus input costs (power, carbon, bauxite). The PS at 0.79x—a 5-year 92nd percentile—reflects a market awarding peak-cycle revenue a historically high multiple, which is a warning flag for quality: the business does not command high multiples across the cycle; the current PS premium is price-driven, not structurally deserved.

## Commodity Cycle Verdict
**Aluminum Price: Elevated but Not Structurally Tight**
SHFE aluminum at 24,360 CNY/t is historically high. The futures curve from 24,290 (Jun 2026) to 24,525 (Nov 2026) shows a mere ~235 CNY contango over 6 months, implying ~0.8% annualized carry. In a genuine structural deficit market, spot prices trade at a premium to futures (backwardation); the flat contango here is consistent with a market pricing current supply as adequate and expecting geopolitical or transient premiums to fade. This does *not* confirm a bearish crash, but it strongly refutes the bull's claim that "the futures curve proves the market expects stable-to-strong prices." The market is paying almost zero premium for future delivery, which is inconsistent with a "supply-constrained super-cycle." The commodity-price layer downgrades the bull thesis from "confirmed" to "unproven."
