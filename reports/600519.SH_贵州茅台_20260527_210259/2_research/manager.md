## Investment Plan: 600519.SH (贵州茅台)

**Rating:** **Overweight**  
*Conviction: Evidence-limited positive view. Thesis has attractive expected value but core pricing and peer data are missing, capping conviction.*

---

### Core Bet (投资主线)
We are betting that Moutai’s defensive cash-flow quality, improving shareholder returns, and early signs of Q1 demand stabilization are being underappreciated. The market is pricing a no-growth scenario (PE ~19.7x, implied profit ~82.7 bn CNY ≈ 2025 actual 82.3 bn), but the company offers a 4%+ dividend yield, zero leverage, RMB 50 bn cash, buybacks, and insider buying. This creates an asymmetric risk/reward: downside is buffered by hard capital-return floors, while any catalyst (channel re-stocking, new retail product traction) could trigger a moderate re-rating.

### Expectation Gap (预期差)
- **Market’s assumption:** Revenue and earnings will remain stagnant or deteriorate further; contract-liability collapse signals a structural demand crack-up; cash conversion is eroding permanently.
- **Our view:** Q1 revenue +6.5% and net profit +1.5% YoY mark a tentative bottom. Contract liabilities (30.3 bn) reflect aggressive channel reform toward direct sales, not a demand free-fall. The FY2025 OCF/net profit dip to 0.75x is partly distorted by related-party deposit movements; Q12026 already recovered to 0.99x. The market is over-extrapolating one bad year while ignoring the balance-sheet strength.

### Company Quality Verdict
**High-quality.** Unmatched brand, 90%+ gross margins, zero financial debt, massive cash pile. However, near-term growth is stalled and the product mix is shifting toward lower-margin series wine. Core franchise remains durable.

### Current Odds Verdict
**Favorable but evidence-limited.** Valuation embeds stagnation, while downside protection via dividends and buybacks is unusually strong for an A-share consumer name. The biggest risk – channel inventory/wholesale price deterioration – cannot be fully assessed because real-time wholesale quotes are missing. Hence, positive stance with capped size.

### Relative Allocation Verdict
**Moderately positive vs. peers, but low confidence.** Peer comparison data is unavailable. Qualitatively, among baijiu names, Moutai’s defensive characteristics (brand, pricing power, balance sheet) are superior. However, we cannot confirm whether a better risk/reward exists in other large-caps like Wuliangye or Luzhou Laojiao due to data gap. Moutai is acceptable but not clearly the best until peer snapshot is restored.

### Management & Capital Allocation Verdict
**Constructive.** Management has moved from a pure growth focus to active shareholder return management: higher dividend payout, inaugural buyback program, and personal insider purchases by the parent group. Capital allocation is disciplined: minimal wasteful capex, no goodwill, large net cash. The investing arm (financial subsidiary) appears modest relative to market cap. This is a positive governance shift.

### Shareholder Structure Verdict
**Stabilizing.** Controlling shareholder (54.4%) increased holdings in late 2025; no pledge overhang. Top-10 concentration is high (~ 68.5%), creating both reduced float and potential institutional over-crowding risk. Overall, ownership structure supports the defensive thesis.

### Investor Communication Verdict
**No live signal.** SSE e-interaction shows no recent replies. Cannot gauge management’s stance on business trends. This is a minor negative for information transparency.

### Policy Direction Verdict
**No direct industry-specific policy.** National plans emphasize green transition, none specifically targeted at baijiu. Consumption policy is indirect; Moutai’s demand is more consumer-led. Policy risk is low.

### Commodity Cycle Verdict
**Critical research gap.** Feitian wholesale price is the single most important operating variable, yet it is missing from all sources. The 1,169 CNY ex-factory price is fixed by the company and does not reflect market equilibrium. Without a reliable wholesale price and channel inventory read, the bull case for demand stabilization cannot be fully validated. This is the primary reason for *capped conviction*.

### Baijiu Channel Verification Verdict
- **Contract liabilities:** Q1 2026 at 30.27 bn is a five-year low, seasonally adjusted down sharply from the peak. This is a bearish red flag *if* not accompanied by rising direct sales. Filing evidence shows direct sales proportion increasing, partially mitigating the concern.
- **Product mix:** Series wine revenue -9.76% YoY, gross margin lower. Negative for total profitability.
- **Wholesale price:** MISSING. No peer basket comparison available.
- **Channel inventory:** Cannot be measured; web searches returned no usable data. **Verdict:** Channel health is uncertain; evidence is insufficient to call a recovery or a collapse.

### Dividend Defensive Verdict
**Medium-grade defensive candidate.** Dividend yield ~4% (based on FY2025 total DPS ~52 CNY). Coverage is adequate (payout ratio ~75%). However, the dividend trap risk exists if operating cash flow does not normalize. For a true defensive yield play, banks or utilities with more predictable earnings might be safer, but Moutai’s brand moat and zero debt still make it a reasonable defensive allocation in the consumer space.

### Investment Action Plan

1. **Initiate a small Overweight position** at current price (~1,303 CNY). Size conservatively: ~1/3 of a full position. The capital return floor (dividend + buyback) provides margin of safety even if the stock drifts.

2. **Monitor key data gap:**
   - Subscribe to a reliable baijiu wholesale price feed (e.g., “今日酒价” or broker channel checks) for Feitian 53° original-carton and loose-bottle prices. Set alerts for changes >5% from estimated current level.
   - Track contract liabilities release in the Q3 report (Oct 2026) and semi-annual report (Aug 2026).

3. **Add to position if:**
   - H1 2026 contract liabilities recover to ≥ 35 bn CNY *and* series wine decline halts.
   - Credible wholesale price data shows stability near recent ranges (e.g., 1,800-2,200 CNY/bottle).
   - OCF ratio in H1 ≥ 0.85x.

4. **Reduce or exit if:**
   - H1 contract liabilities fall below 25 bn CNY *and* wholesale price data confirms a break below 1,500 CNY.
   - Semi-annual OCF ratio stays below 0.8x with no improvement, suggesting deeper structural cash decay.

5. **Peer review:** When daily valuation snapshots become available again, cross-check against Wuliangye (000858.SZ) and Luzhou Laojiao (000568.SZ). If Moutai’s risk/reward is inferior, rotate exposure accordingly.

**Summary:** Buy the cash-flow fortress with a cushion of shareholder returns, but size cautiously due to missing channel-price evidence. The August 2026 half-year report will be the decisive event.
