## 1. Core Bet (What the investor is underwriting)

The investor is betting that the **market has prematurely priced a benign outcome** for a structural business-model shift: from a high-ROE (52%), light-asset, high-growth compounder to a lower-ROE, heavy-asset, medium-growth enterprise. The bet is that capital expenditure (¥3.76bn committed) will compress return on equity, increase depreciation drag, and remove free cash flow visibility, forcing a permanent de-rating of the valuation multiple.

**Falsifiable hypothesis:** If the market is wrong, future earnings will grow fast enough to prevent ROE decay, and contract liabilities will stay elevated, supporting a 28–30x P/E multiple. If the market is right, P/E will settle at 20–24x (or lower) as margins compress and growth decelerates below 19%.

**Bullish evidence (acknowledged but discounted):**

- 24x P/E is below sector median (~42x), though sector median is inflated by loss-making stories.

## 4. Expectation Gap (Is the market already pricing this thesis?)

**Current P/E of 24x** prices in a mild growth deceleration (~15–19% revenue growth) and some margin normalization, but it does **not** fully price a structural ROE decline to 25–30% (implied by capex-heavy depreciation and lower turnover). If ROE falls from 52% to 30%, a 24x P/E on depressed earnings would still be ~15x on peak earnings – but if investors begin discounting lower sustainable earnings, the multiple could compress to 20x.

**Market mood:** The technical downtrend (bearish RSI, multi-month decline) suggests the market has already started to anticipate trouble but has not yet finished marking down the stock to a trough level. The expectation gap favors the bear: the market is probably still too optimistic about the speed of recovery after capex.

## 5. Risk/Reward & Cycle-Valuation Calibration

**Setup classification: High valuation / High prosperity (but prosperity is peaking)**

- Current prosperity: revenue growth 21.5%, ROE 52%, net margin 19%. These are peak-cycle metrics.
- Valuation: 24x P/E is not cheap on a historical basis for a company about to enter a lower-ROE phase. The sector median is irrelevant because it includes high-growth small caps.
- **Fair cycle-valuation check:** A company at peak prosperity with a structural downshift ahead should trade below its historical average multiple (historically ~28–30x). 24x is slightly below that average but does not offer a safety margin for the downside risk.

## 7. Position Sizing & Recommendation

**Rating: Sell**

**Action: Exit current long positions at market (~¥202) or on any bounce toward ¥210–¥215.** Do not initiate new shorts (risk of oversold bounce). For existing holders, reduce to 0% weight.

**Re-entry watch range:** ¥140–¥170 (based on 20–22x P/E on conservative FY26 EPS of ¥7.0–¥7.5). At those levels, the risk/reward improves because the market will have already discounted the capex thesis. No re-entry until the following **minimum conditions** are met:

- Contract liabilities stabilize or rise sequentially for two quarters.
- Revenue growth stabilizes at or above 15%.
- Capex-to-revenue ratio declines from current ~8% toward 4–5%.
- Management explicitly ties new capacity to visible orders (not speculation).

**Conviction level:** Moderate-high (6.5/10). The missing YoY contract liability data prevents maximum conviction. The recommendation is valid as an **expected-value Sell**, not a certainty of large crash.

**Risk management:** If the stock rises above ¥230 without any bullish fundamental catalyst, the sell thesis weakens (market may be re-rating the capex as growth-positive). At that point, re-evaluate with updated capex ROIC data.
