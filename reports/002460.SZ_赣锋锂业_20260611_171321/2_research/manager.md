## Investment Plan: 002460.SZ 赣锋锂业

**Rating: Overweight** (Evidence-Limited)

This is a cyclical wager, not a quality-compounding ticket. The bull thesis bets on a lithium-cycle inflection where earnings recovery and a second-curve narrative converge, while the bear thesis argues the market has already priced a fragile recovery into a stretched multiple, with Q2 earnings showing a sharp sequential collapse. After auditing the full debate against the filing, commodity, and market context, the bull case has a tradable expectation gap, but only on a forward-normalized earnings bridge, not on trailing TTM. Uncertainty is high: the Q2 profit cliff (net profit ~6bn vs 18.4bn in Q1) is a live falsification signal that neither side has fully closed. The Overweight rating reflects a positive expected value that is material but conditional—it requires the Q2 earnings quality breakdown and Q3 contract-liability follow-through to remain intact. This is a staged-building posture, not an all-in conviction Buy.

### Company Quality Verdict

Ganfeng is a vertically integrated lithium platform spanning upstream resources, midstream lithium chemicals, and downstream battery/energy storage. The upstream resource base (Goulamina spodumene, Argentine brine projects) provides a structural cost-curve advantage once ramped, and the downstream battery segment offers a second growth curve that pure-play miners lack. However, company quality is moderated by three hard facts: (1) trailing ROE is low at ~3.7% and ROA at ~2.25%, reflecting the trough of a brutal downcycle; (2) gross margin at ~30% significantly lags Tianqi Lithium (~63%), indicating a less scarce resource mix; and (3) the balance sheet carries 55.3% debt-to-assets, with management repeatedly accessing equity, convertible, and debt markets. The business is decent but not elite—its quality rank sits in the middle of the lithium peer group, behind Tianqi on pure asset quality but ahead of Huayou Cobalt on upstream resource integration. The investment case does not rest on quality compounding; it rests on the cycle.

### Current Odds Verdict

The market is pricing a specific, testable assumption: **that around half of Q1's profitability was temporary, and the normalized earnings power is roughly 50bn/year, not 80-87bn.** At Friday's close of 69.94 and a TTM PE of 38.5x, the quote already reflects a heavy cyclical discount. The bull case argues forward normalized earnings could be 60-87bn (PE of 16.9x-24.4x), giving positive expectation if lithium stays above 17万. The bear case argues Q2's sequential profit collapse to ~6bn validates that Q1 was an anomaly, and that a lithium-price retreat toward 12-13万 would drop annual earnings to 30-40bn (PE of 36-49x), making the stock expensive. The odds sit roughly 55/45 in favor of the bull scenario over a 6-12 month horizon, but that edge is narrow. The key is that even the conservative 60bn annualized profit case (which Q2's 6bn does not automatically refute if Q1 had one-off working-capital timing or seasonal mix effects) yields a mid-20s PE that is acceptable for a cyclical recovering from trough. The probability-weighted payoff is positive but modest compared to pure beta plays like Tianqi.

### Relative Allocation Verdict

Within the lithium chain, upstream resources currently show the strongest median metrics (PE 46.8x, ROE 4.1%, profit growth 277%). Among lithium peers, **Tianqi Lithium (002466.SZ) is a better pure lithium-beta expression**: it has higher gross margins (62.7%), a lower PB (2.25x), higher ROA (6.24%), and a stronger v4_score (71.7 vs 55.9). If the core bet is simply "lithium price stays elevated," Tianqi is the superior vehicle. However, Ganfeng offers something Tianqi does not: a downstream battery/energy-storage segment that is growing as a share of revenue (35.5% of H1 2025 revenue vs 28.4% previously) and could command a different valuation multiple if profitability is proven. The allocation decision depends on the investor's mandate: **for a concentrated lithium-cycle bet, Tianqi is better; for a barbell of cycle recovery plus optionality on energy-storage growth, Ganfeng is a legitimate build candidate.** Huayou Cobalt (603799.SH) screens cheaper on PE (12.3x) and PB (1.8x) but has higher leverage (63.6% debt ratio) and lower gross margin (20.7%), making it a riskier expression of the same recovery. Ganfeng is the acceptable middle: not the cheapest, not the highest-quality, but the most diversified.

### Management & Capital Allocation Verdict

Chairman Li Liangbin and Vice Chairman Wang Xiaoshen are founder-operators with deep industry tenure. Capital allocation shows a heavy reinvestment bias: cumulative capex of 7.4bn in FY2025 and 9.2bn in FY2024, funded by a mix of operating cash, debt, H-share placements, and convertible bonds. The equity-incentive programs (employee stock ownership plans with multiple vesting tranches) align management with shareholders but also add dilution risk. Recent pledge activity (Li Liangbin and Wang Xiaoshen both have active pledges) is a yellow flag, not a red one: there is no evidence of forced selling, and some older pledges have been released. The company's pattern of seeding, holding, and occasionally exiting minority investments (investees include mining, technology, and financial interests across the lithium chain) suggests a repeatable capital-allocation capability, though the aggregate carrying value of 11.7bn in long-term equity investments needs more disclosure on project-level returns. Overall, stewardship is acceptable for a growth-phase industrial company, but the financing intensity means shareholders are funding the build-out as much as management is compounding existing capital.

### Shareholder Structure Verdict

Ownership is founder-anchored but with a wide retail base. Li Liangbin (18.1%) and Wang Xiaoshen (6.7%) collectively hold ~25% of total shares, and HKSCC Nominees (23.0%) primarily represents H-share holders rather than a single strategic investor. The A-share holder count has surged from 299k in early April to 332k by late May—a 33k increase in under two months. This retail influx during a 23% price decline from the Q1 high of ~91 to 69.94 is a classic "institutional distribution into retail hands" pattern. The top-10 float holders are dominated by passive index funds (CSI Nonferrous Metals ETF, Huatai-PineBridge CSI 300 ETF), not active institutional accumulators. Pledge ratios are elevated but not extreme, and no imminent large unlock event appears in the data. The structure is a headwind for near-term price momentum: even if the fundamental thesis is correct, the stock may need time to consolidate the dispersed holder base before a sustained re-rating can occur.

### Policy Direction Verdict

The 15th Five-Year Plan (2025) and the 2024 Comprehensive Green Transition Directive both place lithium-battery supply chains inside national strategic priorities for renewable energy and electrification. This provides durable multi-year tailwind for industry TAM. However, policy support is industry-wide, not company-specific. Ganfeng's competitive moat within that TAM depends on its cost curve and downstream customer relationships, not on policy licenses. The transmission path from policy to earnings is indirect: policy supports EV and energy-storage demand, which supports lithium volumes and prices, which flows into Ganfeng's revenue. This is directionally constructive but should not be priced as a company-specific catalyst without order, contract, or pricing evidence.

### Thematic Valuation Bridge

**Core Valuation (Tier-1):**
- **Order Ramp (contract liabilities):** Filing-backed. Q1 2026 contract liabilities of 1.94bn, up 5.1% QoQ from FY2025's 1.85bn. Q2 (H1) contract liabilities of 2.14bn, up 10.2% QoQ. Management confirms "orders ample." This supports revenue visibility into H2 2026 and can enter base-case revenue estimates, but with a haircut: contract liabilities/revenue ratio fell from 21.1% in Q1 to 10.4% in H1, so the order book is growing in absolute terms but not keeping pace with revenue expansion. The bull interpretation ("demand converting to revenue") and bear interpretation ("customers unwilling to prepay at same ratio") are both live. Treat as a positive but incomplete signal.

**Scenario Valuation (Tier-2):**
- **Overseas Expansion (Argentina brine, Mali Goulamina):** Filing-backed. Goulamina Phase I has commenced production; Argentine projects are advancing. Successful ramp would lower the consolidated cost curve and improve gross margin toward 35-40%. This is a 12-24 month catalyst. Keep in upside scenario until cost-savings evidence appears.
- **Energy Storage / Data Center Backup Power:** Mentioned in annual report text as a growth driver for lithium demand, particularly from energy-storage battery shipments. No separate revenue, profit, or order disclosure exists. This is a qualitative optionality that supports the downstream battery segment's TAM narrative but cannot enter core valuation.

**Narrative Options (Tier-3):**
- **AI / Computing Infrastructure Backup Power:** A single mention in the annual report. No disclosed customer, order, or revenue. This is a market-imagination premium at best; assign zero core value.

**Primary Investment NAV:**
- The company holds 11.7bn in long-term equity investments across mining, technology, and financial entities. The Shenzhen Luyuan Mining Investment, Jiangxi Zhili Technology, and other holdings appear in filing text but lack individual carrying values, exit timelines, or IPO catalysts. A conservative treatment applies the consolidated carrying value with a 20-30% liquidity haircut, adding roughly 8-9bn to SOTP value beyond operating earnings. This is not separately driving the rating but provides a modest NAV floor. The repeatable pattern of seeding investees across the lithium chain, some of which have realized gains in prior periods, suggests management's first-level investing capability deserves a qualitative bull factor rather than being dismissed as one-off.

### Commodity Cycle Verdict

GFEX lithium carbonate futures (LC2609, the most active contract) closed at 174,600 CNY/ton on June 11, 2026. This represents a 72.8% rise from the trough approximately six months ago but remains 71% below the 2022 peak of ~600,000. The forward curve is essentially flat through November 2026 (174,600-175,900), indicating the futures market expects price stability around 17-18万 rather than either a renewed surge or a sharp retreat. This is the single most important macro fact for the thesis: **the commodity price backdrop is supportive but not explosive.** Ganfeng's realized selling price will lag the futures proxy, and spodumene concentrate costs are not observable in the supplied data. A critical research gap is the absence of a verified concentrate benchmark or company-disclosed unit cost structure. Without it, we cannot precisely model the margin bridge from lithium price to company gross profit. The proxy evidence supports the "stabilized at a sustainable level" portion of the bull thesis but does not confirm margin expansion.

### Metals / Mining Verification Verdict

The metals/mining gate is triggered. As a lithium resource and processing company, Ganfeng must be valued on mine-asset quality, cost curve, and project execution, not on EV/battery growth multiples. The key evidence gaps are material: (1) no reserve/resource tonnage with grade and equity-output breakdowns is supplied; (2) no AISC or all-in cost data is available; (3) no project-level ramp schedule with capex-to-production timelines is verified. The GFEX futures proxy is useful for direction but cannot replace realized-price and cost-bridge data. The company's segment disclosure splits "Basic Chemical Materials" (lithium chemicals, 56.8% of H1 2025 revenue) from "Lithium Batteries and Cells" (35.5%), which is adequate for segment-level valuation. The mining-specific conviction cap is material: without grade, cost, and volume evidence, the bull thesis on margin sustainability relies more on inference than hard facts. The Overweight rating acknowledges this gap.

### Filing Internal Quality Verdict

Filing coverage is strong: four reports (Q1 2026, FY2025, Q3 2025, H1 2025) with full text extraction, and all nine core-underwriting questions answered. Key internal signals:

- **Cash-flow quality:** Q1 2026 OCF/net profit of 0.43x is a yellow flag. Q2 (H1) improvement to 0.71x is positive but reflects a 6bn profit quarter vs 18.4bn in Q1—the ratio improved on a smaller numerator. Receivables/revenue improved from 32.9% to 19.3% (Q1) and further to 18.6% (H1), indicating better collection. Inventory/revenue fell from 39.5% to 32.7% (H1), but absolute inventory rose from 130bn to 157bn and turnover days increased from ~75 to ~84. This combination—improving ratios, growing absolute stock, longer turnover—is consistent with a product-mix shift toward batteries (which carry longer production cycles) but is not verified by segment-level inventory disclosure.
- **Non-recurring items:** Footnotes mention credit impairment losses and asset-disposal gains. The magnitude relative to Q1's 18.4bn net profit is not separately quantified in the supplied data. This is a live bear risk: if Q1 contained material one-off gains, the annualized earnings bridge is overstated.
- **MD&A text change:** The FY2025 annual report added explicit language on risk management including hedging, FX, and commodity-price volatility. This is not a red flag but indicates management considers price risk material.
- **Balance-sheet signals:** Contract liabilities growing for two consecutive quarters; long-term equity investments stable at 11.6-11.7bn; goodwill minimal at 182mn. Net debt is elevated but serviceable given Q1 interest expense of 2.7bn vs FY2025 full-year 15.3bn.

### Earnings-Model Bridge

**Bull Case (probability ~35%, payoff ~+40-60%):**
- Lithium carbonate stays at 17-19万/ton throughout 2026-2027.
- Annualized net profit of 80-87bn (seasonality-adjusted Q1 extrapolation) holds, implying 2026 full-year EPS of ~4.0-4.2 CNY.
- Battery segment profitability improves as scale grows and raw-material costs stabilize.
- Target PE of 20-25x on 80bn net profit → market cap of 1,600-2,000bn → share price of 76-95 CNY.
- OCF/net profit recovers to >0.8x by Q3.

**Base Case (probability ~40%, payoff ~+10-25%):**
- Lithium carbonate oscillates between 15-18万/ton.
- Full-year 2026 net profit of 55-65bn, reflecting H1's 24.4bn plus a modest H2.
- PE of 22-27x on 60bn → market cap of 1,320-1,620bn → share price of 63-77 CNY.
- Contract liabilities remain stable, and Q3 cash conversion stays above 0.6x.

**Bear Case (probability ~25%, payoff ~-20-35%):**
- Lithium retreats to 12-14万/ton as restarted capacity or demand slowdown hits.
- Full-year net profit of 30-40bn.
- PE compresses to 15-20x on cyclical earnings decline → market cap of 450-800bn → share price of 21-38 CNY.
- Inventory write-downs resume, and OCF turns negative.

The base and bull cases together support a positive expected value. The bear case is severe but requires a lithium-price retreat that the futures curve does not currently anticipate. The Overweight rating weights the base case most heavily while acknowledging the bear tail risk is live.

### Growth Sustainability Verdict

Revenue growth is undergoing a V-shaped recovery driven by price, not volume. H1 2026 revenue of 205.3bn annualizes to ~410bn, nearly double FY2025's 230.8bn. The drivers: (1) lithium price recovery (verified via GFEX futures), (2) volume recovery from trough production levels (inferred from inventory build and management commentary), and (3) battery-segment revenue expansion (verified via segment disclosure, now 35.5% of revenue). Sustainability hinges on three conditions: lithium prices not falling below 15万, battery segment gross margin reaching disclosure threshold, and contract liabilities not declining in Q3. Falsification would come from Q3 contract liabilities dropping below 18bn, Q3 revenue growth decelerating below 20% QoQ, or lithium futures breaking below 15万.

### Data Coverage and Evidence Gaps

Two critical research gaps cap conviction:

1. **No company-disclosed unit cost or realized selling price for lithium products.** GFEX futures are a proxy; they show direction but not the realized margin. Without this, the margin-bridge from lithium price to company profit is an inference, not a fact.

2. **No battery-segment standalone profitability.** The segment is 35.5% of revenue but gross margin, operating margin, and cash conversion are not separately disclosed. The bull thesis that this is a "second growth curve" that deserves a higher multiple cannot be proven or disproven from current filings.

The Overweight rating incorporates these gaps as conviction caps. They do not negate the thesis, but they prevent a Buy rating.

### Final Action Plan

- **Rating:** Overweight (Evidence-Limited)
- **Core Bet:** Lithium cycle has bottomed; 17-18万 lithium supports 55-65bn annualized earnings, giving a forward PE of 22-27x—acceptable for a recovering cyclical with a downstream growth option.
- **Why Not Buy:** Q2 profit collapse from 18.4bn to ~6bn is a live risk; contract-liability/revenue ratio is declining; battery segment margins are unproven; retail holders are accumulating into price weakness. These cap conviction below "strong conviction in the bull thesis."
- **Why Not Hold:** The balance of evidence—contract liabilities rising in absolute terms, lithium futures curve flat at 17-18万, receivables and inventory ratios improving, OCF recovering, and the downstream battery business growing—tilts positive. The expected value is attractive enough to warrant a staged overweight.
- **Position Sizing:** Initiate up to 1/3 of target position at current levels (~70). Add second 1/3 between 60-66 if offered. Hold final 1/3 for post-Q3 confirmation.
- **Time Horizon:** 6-12 months, with a hard reassessment at Q3 2026 report.
- **Exit Triggers:** Lithium futures below 12万; Q3 contract liabilities below 16bn; FY2026 guidance or run-rate below 40bn net profit.
