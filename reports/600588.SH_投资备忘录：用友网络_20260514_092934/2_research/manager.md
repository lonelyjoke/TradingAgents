### 1. The Core Bets and the Key Unverified Assumptions

The bull thesis is a bet on **gross margin expansion driven by the tail end of a costly cloud-platform transition**, with the up-front investment converting into high-margin recurring revenue. The bear thesis is a bet on **structural margin deterioration**—that the negative gross margins are not a transition cost but evidence of a broken business model, and that cash consumption will accelerate.

Both sides have marshalled financial numbers, but **none of these numbers are verified against primary filings or tool outputs in this round**. The debate relies entirely on analyst assertions. Therefore, every concrete percentage, cash flow figure, and balance-sheet line must be treated as an **unverified key assumption** for the purpose of this decision. This does not make the debate useless, but it caps conviction and demands conditional planning.

The specific unverified claims critical to the bull case:
- 2025 full-year OCF of +¥812M, vs -¥274M in 2024
- 2025 EBITDA of +¥190M
- Contract liabilities of ¥3.2B (2025)
- 2025 full-year gross margin of -20%, improving from -25.8% in 2024
- Q1 2026 gross margin of -56.9%
- Revenue stagnation: ¥9.26B in 2022 to ¥9.18B in 2025
- Net cash moving from +¥633M in 2024 to -¥302M in 2025
- Working capital widening from -¥1.33B to -¥2.73B

Without verification, the debate is a clash of interpretations of assumed numbers. I will base my rating on the **internal logic and the pattern of evidence**, while flagging the exact data that must be confirmed.

### 2. Expectation Gap and Cycle-Valuation Calibration

The stock is currently in a **high-valuation/low-prosperity** quadrant: negative net income, negative gross margins, negligible revenue growth, but a market cap (~¥38B) that implies a premium for an eventual turnaround. Under our framework, high valuation/low prosperity requires a clear future inflection or scarce growth to justify anything above Hold. The question is whether that inflection is plausible enough to make the expected value positive.

The bull’s expectation gap is that the market is pricing a 90% probability of failure, while the actual probability of a successful margin turnaround is 40% or higher. The bear argues the market is still underestimating the cash burn and the structural nature of the losses. **My assessment is that the bull’s expectation gap is plausible in theory but heavily reliant on unverified assumptions about contract quality, cloud adoption, and the absence of further cost shocks.** The bear’s expectation gap is better supported by the direction of the assumed financials—four years of negative gross margins, revenue stagnation, and a widening working-capital deficit.

The bull’s appeal to industry AI tailwinds is a **proxy argument at best** and an **unverified assumption** at worst. No evidence was presented that Yonyou’s YonGPT is generating meaningful revenue or margin uplift. Alibaba Cloud’s growth is not a direct proxy for a legacy ERP provider’s ability to monetise AI. This argument cannot carry valuation weight without firm-specific data.

### 3. Evidence Gaps and Their Consequences

The following evidence gaps are decisive for conviction:

| Gap | Why It Matters | Bull’s Implication | Bear’s Implication |
|-----|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Contract liability composition** (SaaS subscription vs. low-margin project implementation) | The entire bull thesis of future high-margin revenue rests on the assumption that the growing contract liabilities are predominantly high-margin cloud subscriptions. | Treats all contract liabilities as a “high-margin entry ticket.” | Argues the growth is driven by low-margin/negative-margin project work, consistent with declining margins. |
| **Gross margin trajectory beyond Q1 2026** | The Q1 -57% print (if real) could be a seasonal trough or a sign of accelerating cost blow-out. | Claims it is seasonal, anchored to full-year -20% improvement. | Claims it is a structural slide, part of a four-year trend. |
| **YonGPT AI revenue contribution** | The AI catalyst is central to the bull’s narrative that the market is mispricing the stock. | Cites industry AI growth as proxy; no company-level data. | Labels it an unverified assumption; no evidence of monetisation. |
| **Management guidance on capex, conversion timeline, and profitability** | The bull’s bet on an imminent inflection requires confirmation that the heavy investment phase is ending. | Implicitly assumes the investment cycle is peaking. | Argues there is no sign of a slowdown in cash consumption. |

Given these gaps, **the bull thesis is evidence-limited**. A high-conviction Buy would be irresponsible. The bear thesis is also evidence-limited in that it cannot prove the Q1 margin is the new normal, but the multi-year trend of negative margins and deteriorating working capital provides a stronger factual backdrop, even if the exact numbers are unverified.

### 4. Probability/Payoff Assessment and Expected Value

I will construct a probability-weighted scenario analysis using the unverified numbers as the debate’s assumed baseline, acknowledging that these numbers must be verified before any actual execution. The bull’s 40/70/15 probability splits are overly optimistic; the bear’s 55/35/10 are more aligned with the pattern of financial deterioration, but I will calibrate my own:

- **Bear case (structural decline, liquidity stress within 12–18 months): 45%.** Assumes gross margins stay deeply negative, cash burn forces a dilutive capital raise or debt restructuring. Stock drops 40–50% to ¥6–8.
- **Muddle-through case (chronic weak profitability, no surge in growth): 40%.** Margins slowly improve to -10% to -15%, revenue remains flat, contract liabilities inch up. Stock oscillates between ¥10–14, with no material upside after opportunity cost. Expected return near zero.
- **Bull case (successful cloud transition, margins inflect to positive within 2 years): 15%.** Requires management to cut capex, show SaaS ARR growth, and deliver gross margins above -5% in 2026. Stock could re-rate towards ¥18–20.

**Expected value (from ¥12):**  
(0.45 × -40%) + (0.40 × +5%) + (0.15 × +60%) = -18% + 2% + 9% = **-7%** expected return over a 12-month horizon. This is a negative expected value, even with generous assumptions about the bull-case payoff. The downside risk is substantial, and the most probable scenarios do not compensate for it.

The bull’s central argument that “the 3.2B contract liability cushions the downside” is flawed if those contracts are uneconomic to fulfil—the bear correctly notes that contract liabilities are future obligations, not free cash. The widening working-capital deficit suggests that fulfilling those contracts is consuming more cash than the prepayments provide, which is a solvency red flag, not a sign of strength.

---

### 5. Rating Decision: Underweight

**Rating: Underweight**

The negative thesis has positive expected value, while the bull’s positive expected value relies on unverified, and increasingly strained, assumptions about contract quality and cost control. The evidence gaps are too large to justify a Buy or Overweight rating, but are not so one-sided as to demand an immediate Sell (the company might yet muddle through without a catastrophic cash crunch). **Underweight is the appropriate risk posture:** it reflects a cautious view, recommends trimming exposure, and captures the asymmetry favouring further downside.

Preference: Underweight over Hold, because the bear case is more consistent with the multi-year trend of the unverified financials, while the bull case requires a sharp reversal that has not yet appeared. Hold would imply the evidence is balanced, but the balance tilts toward continued value erosion until concrete proof of margin improvement emerges.

### 6. Actionable Investment Plan

**For existing holders:**
- **Reduce position** to a tactical underweight (no more than 1-2% of portfolio, from the bull’s suggested 3–5%). This limits downside exposure while keeping a minimal stake to monitor the thesis.
- If the position is unprofitable and no catalyst is imminent, consider exiting entirely to avoid further opportunity cost.

**For traders without a position:**
- **Do not initiate a long.** The risk/reward is unattractive on the available (unverified) evidence.
- A short position could be justified only with verified data on cash burn and contract-liability quality, and with a clear stop level above ¥14.

**Falsification triggers and re-rating conditions:**
1. **Verify the actual 2025 annual report and Q1 2026 financials.** If the reported gross margin, OCF, and net cash figures deviate significantly from the debate numbers, re-run the analysis.
2. **Critical upgrade trigger:** If H1 2026 (semi-annual) reported gross margin recovers to **better than -10%**, **OCF is positive** for the half year, and **contract liabilities grow alongside accelerating total revenue** (not just flat revenue with more prepayments), then the bear case weakens materially. At that point, upgrade to **Hold** and consider a staged **Overweight** entry if subscription revenue composition is disclosed and improving.
3. **Critical downgrade trigger:** If Q2 2026 gross margin is **worse than -30%**, or net cash worsens by another ¥1B, or the company announces a capital raise, then upgrade the bearish conviction to **Sell**.
4. **Data to demand:** Request a breakdown of contract liabilities by product type (SaaS subscription vs. implementation), YonGPT-specific revenue or customer count, and management’s capex guidance for 2026–2027. Without these, the bull thesis remains an unverified assumption.

### 7. Final Remarks

The debate was rigorous but exposed that both the bull and bear are trading on **unverified numbers and key assumptions**. The bull’s narrative is emotionally compelling but analytically fragile—it assumes that the cloud transition’s heavy costs are about to end, that contract liabilities are high-quality, and that an AI windfall will arrive, none of which can be substantiated with the information at hand. Until the evidence gaps are closed, the prudent stance is to reduce risk.

**The market may eventually be proven wrong about Yonyou’s demise, but the data to prove that is not yet in the public domain. The burden of proof lies with the bulls, and they have not met it.**
