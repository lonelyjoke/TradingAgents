### Core Reasoning

The bull thesis rests on an **unverified central assumption** – that unit cost declines are outpacing unit price declines (single-ticket cost > single-ticket revenue). Without this micro data, the reported profit acceleration (+94% Q1 2026 YoY) could equally reflect aggressive volume discounting and unsustainable cost compression. The **balance sheet is deteriorating materially**: net debt expanded from -29.7bn (end 2024) to -45.7bn (Q1 2026), while free cash flow (OCF – Capex) covered only 13% of capex in FY2025. The industry is standardized, low-switching-cost, and historically prone to prisoner’s-dilemma pricing. The market correctly prices scepticism: the stock’s P/E is at a 5-year 2.5th percentile, but its P/B is at the 77th percentile – a hallmark of potential value traps where assets are not cheap given low returns.

Expected value is only **~+10%** in a realistic scenario distribution (15% bull / 45% base / 40% bear), with negative skew and dependence on missing evidence. The risk/reward is unattractive for a high-beta, leveraged cyclical in a market (CSI 500 P/E @ 99.7th percentile) that leaves no margin for error.
