**Rating:** **Underweight**  
**Conviction:** Moderate  
**Core Decision:** Reduce exposure at current levels (~¥25), unless and until the upcoming H1 2026 report provides clear evidence that gross margins have stabilized and operating leverage is converting revenue into sustainable profit growth. The bull thesis rests on an unverified critical assumption—that the decline in unit prices has ended—while the bear thesis is better supported by the preponderance of verified financial trends.

### 1. Thesis Evaluation & Expectation Gap

**Bull core bet:** The ¥21.06B contract liabilities lock in massive revenue growth; fixed-cost absorption (operating leverage) will drive absolute profit growth even if margins stay at current levels; the market is mispricing this “certainty of profit growth,” creating a asymmetric long opportunity.

**Bear core bet:** Revenue certainty does not equal profit certainty. Margins remain under structural pressure from buyer concentration and overcapacity. Historical quarterly patterns show that operating margins deteriorate sharply after Q1, making the extrapolation of Q1 margins unreliable. The stock’s 34x P/E already embeds ambitious recovery expectations that are likely to be disappointed.

**Verdict after reviewing all exchanges:**  
- The bull argument is **structurally dependent on one unverified assumption**: that the gross margin has bottomed and will not slide further (e.g., back toward 14% or below). No verifiable product-price, bidding-price, or order-book data was provided to support this. The bull’s own “time and rhythm” argument implicitly concedes that further margin erosion is possible but deferred.  
- The bear systematically dismantled the “operating leverage” narrative by showing that selling & administrative expenses are not fixed but grew 15% YoY, and that 2025’s full-year derivative operating margin (2.38%) was far below Q1’s 6.73%—a pattern that warns against annualizing Q1 2026’s 6.21%. This is a **verified historical fact, not opinion.**  
- The bull’s probability-weighted expected return is highly sensitive to the prior probabilities assigned to the “optimistic” and “pessimistic” scenarios. When adjusted to reflect the recent downward margin trend and the lack of stabilization evidence, the expected return becomes marginally negative or barely positive, with a maximum downside of ~36% in a severe scenario vs. a maximum upside of ~52% in an optimistic scenario that now carries low conviction.

**Expectation gap:** The market has already priced in a significant recovery narrative (stock up 3.5x from lows, fell to 25x, still trading at 34x P/E on thin margins). To justify further upside, H1 2026 must deliver stronger margin stability than the current trajectory suggests. The risk/reward is therefore skewed negatively until that data arrives.
