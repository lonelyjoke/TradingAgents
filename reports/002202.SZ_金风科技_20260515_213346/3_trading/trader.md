### 1. Core Thesis & Expectation Gap

**Core bear bet:**  
Revenue momentum is real, but profit certainty is not. Gross margin has been in a verified structural downtrend (14% full‑year 2025 vs. 16.76% in Q1 2026 – a level that history shows is not sustainable through the full year). The market is paying 34x P/E for a business that delivered only 2.38% derivative operating margin in 2025, and Q1 2026’s 6.21% margin is likely to compress as the year progresses. The bull case requires an **unverified assumption** – that turbine bidding prices have stopped falling – and ignores the verified pattern of sequential margin deterioration after Q1.

**Expectation gap:**  
The 3.5x recovery from lows and still‑elevated P/E already price in a successful margin‑stabilization story. H1 2026 will either confirm or break that story. Until confirmation arrives, the risk/reward is skewed to the downside. The market is too optimistic about the durability of Q1 margin levels.

**Falsification signals:**  
- **Bull case fails** if H1 2026 gross margin ≤14.5% or derivative operating margin <5%.  
- **Bear case weakens** if H1 2026 gross margin holds above 16% **and** sequentially improves, and new‑order pricing data (when available) shows a floor.
