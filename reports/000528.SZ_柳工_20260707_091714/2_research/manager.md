# Research Manager Accepted Underwriting Model

**Rating**: Overweight

**Research Readiness**: partial

**Core Bet**: We are underwriting that the domestic construction machinery replacement cycle is genuinely starting, visible through early orders and ASP firming, and that the market has over-discounted a temporary margin/forex headwind while ignoring a structurally higher overseas profit pool. The single variable that must be right: domestic machinery revenue growth remains positive (>5% YoY) through 2027E, disproving the 'zero-growth' bear case.

### 本报告要回答的关键问题

1. Is the domestic replacement cycle real and sustainable, or is it a one-off restocking that will reverse?
2. Can consolidated gross margin recover from Q1-2026's 21.3% toward 22.5–23.5%, or is structural domestic competition permanently capping profitability?
3. Is the Q1 OCF deterioration purely seasonal/forex-driven, or does it signal a systemic cash-conversion problem that threatens dividends and forces de-rating?
4. Is Liugong's low valuation (PE 10.8x, PB 0.89x) a genuine margin of safety, or a value trap justified by inferior returns and weak cash quality?
5. Why own Liugong over cleaner, higher-ROE peers like Anhui Heli or Shantui if the sector cycle is recovering?

### 核心问题裁决

**Q1：Is the domestic replacement cycle real and sustainable, or is it a one-off restocking that will reverse?**
- **为何关键：** Without volume growth, the operating leverage and margin-recovery story collapses; the bear's zero-growth case would be correct.
- **当前结论：** Early-cycle signals are genuine but not yet conclusive. Contract liabilities up 43.8% YoY and industry excavator sales up ~39% in May-2026 indicate a demand pulse that is unlikely to be pure channel stuffing. However, industry cycle scan labels the cycle stage 'evidence insufficient', and the exact split between domestic terminal demand and 'domestic-to-export' transfer is unverified. We accept a base-case of moderate domestic growth (+8–10% in 2026E) but require H1-2026 disclosure to confirm conversion of contract liabilities into revenue without a simultaneous spike in channel inventory or receivables.
- **采用证据：** KPE01 (2026-06-12): broker channel check reporting 39% domestic excavator sales growth in May, noted some domestic-to-export factor.；KPE02 (2026-06-29): sell-side note reporting May excavator total +36% YoY, domestic +39%, export +34%.；EV011 (Q1-2026 financials): contract liabilities CNY 744.9 mn vs CNY 517.9 mn, +43.8% YoY.；Industry Cycle Scan: verdict 'cycle evidence insufficient'; no hard utilization or de-stocking data.
- **最强反证：** Bear's argument that contract-liability growth accompanied a 32% surge in short-term borrowings, suggesting forced channel financing rather than genuine end-demand. No independent dealer inventory data is available to resolve this.
- **模型/估值影响：** Domestic engineering-machinery revenue growth set to +10% (2026E), +8% (2027E), +5% (2028E) in base case. If growth is zero, 2027E EPS drops from CNY 1.12 toward CNY 0.85–0.95, removing ~25% of base-case fair value. Bull case adds ~CNY 0.14 EPS if 12% growth materialises.
- **置信度与下次验证：** medium；H1-2026 interim report (by end-Aug 2026): domestic/international segment revenue, contract liability conversion into revenue, commentary on channel inventory, and any revision to full-year outlook. Monthly CCMA excavator sales data through Q3-2026.

**Q2：Can consolidated gross margin recover from Q1-2026's 21.3% toward 22.5–23.5%, or is structural domestic competition permanently capping profitability?**
- **为何关键：** Gross margin is the dominant driver of EPS variance: a 1pp move changes 2027E EPS by ~CNY 0.05–0.06, and the bull/bear gap on margin alone separates a 12x stock from an 8x stock.
- **当前结论：** Partial recovery to ~22.0% in 2027E is more likely than a sustained decline toward 21% or a rapid snap-back to 24%. Q1 margin compression was led by a one-off forex spike (finance-expense ratio +2.15pp) rather than core manufacturing cost pressure. Management has announced price hikes (KPE03), though these are unconfirmed in official filings. Overseas gross margin (29.73%) continues to mix upward as overseas revenue grows faster. However, domestic gross margin (15.75% in FY2025, declining) shows no pricing power, and until H1 data confirms the price hikes stick, the margin recovery remains a scenario assumption, not a verified fact.
- **采用证据：** EV014–EV016 (Q1-2026 margin data): consolidated gross margin 21.31% (-0.70pp YoY), operating margin 6.97% (-1.74pp), net margin 6.02% (-1.16pp).；EV030 (FY2025 segment profitability): overseas gross margin 29.73% (+1.16pp), domestic 15.75% (-1.63pp).；KPE03 (2026-06-12): sell-side note claiming excavator, crane, electric-loader price hikes, 'some regions already implemented, full rollout expected H2 2026'.；KPE04 (2026-06-15): sell-side weekly noting Q2 forex drag materially reduced relative to Q1.
- **最强反证：** Price-hike evidence is purely sell-side narrative (promotional_private_unverified) with zero official confirmation in filings or exchange announcements. Domestic gross margin has been declining for at least three years. The bear's argument that competitive intensity prevents sustainable margin expansion domestically cannot be dismissed without official disclosure.
- **模型/估值影响：** Base-case consolidated gross margin: 21.5% (2026E), 22.0% (2027E), 23.0% (2028E). Bull case: +1.5pp vs base in 2027E. Bear case: 21.0% flat through 2028E. 2027E base EPS CNY 1.12 vs bull CNY 1.29 vs bear CNY 0.67. Gross margin alone explains ~60% of the bull/bear EPS spread.
- **置信度与下次验证：** medium；H1-2026 report: consolidated and segment gross margins, management commentary on pricing environment, disclosure of price-hike implementation rate. Q2-specific financials to isolate whether the Q1 margin trough was seasonal or structural.

**Q3：Is the Q1 OCF deterioration purely seasonal/forex-driven, or does it signal a systemic cash-conversion problem that threatens dividends and forces de-rating?**
- **为何关键：** The bear's most damaging argument is that poor cash conversion (OCF/NI = -0.90 in Q1, 0.62 in FY2025) makes the dividend yield a trap. If true, the 'defensive dividend' thesis evaporates and the stock loses its valuation floor.
- **当前结论：** Cash conversion is genuinely weak and is the most serious unresolved risk in the thesis. Q1 OCF was hit by a triple whammy: (a) seasonal working-capital build typical for construction machinery, (b) a CNY 192 mn forex-driven finance expense spike, and (c) a CNY 94 mn credit-impairment charge suggesting some downstream stress. The dividend (CNY 0.325 DPS proposed) is still covered by FY2025 OCF (1.5x coverage), but the margin of safety is thin. If 2026E OCF remains below CNY 1.0 bn while capex stays at CNY 700 mn+, FCF will be near zero or negative, and the buyback may be the first item sacrificed, then the dividend. We assign a 25–30% probability of a dividend cut within 12 months, which limits the 'defensive' label but does not yet make it a trap.
- **采用证据：** EV005 (Q1-2026 balance sheet): short-term borrowings surged 32% QoQ to CNY 7.53 bn; derivative financial liabilities +11,191% to CNY 10.6 mn.；EV011 (Q1-2026 cash flow): OCF/net profit ratio = -0.90; credit impairment loss -CNY 93.7 mn vs -CNY 113.2 mn (Q1-2025).；FY2025 annual report: OCF CNY 1,005 mn, net profit CNY 1,609 mn, OCF/NI = 0.62.；Capital allocation: 2026-07-01 share buyback announcement (up to CNY 400 mn); FY2025 dividend proposal CNY 0.325/share (~CNY 662 mn total).；Dividend Defensive Context: rating 'strong' but stability 'watch'; coverage 'pass' based on FY2025, not forward.
- **最强反证：** The bull correctly identifies that the CNY 10.6 mn derivative liability position indicates active hedging initiation in Q1, and that CNY has weakened since April, potentially reversing some of the Q1 forex loss in Q2. The buyback announcement (July 2026) signals management confidence in liquidity. But these are mitigating rather than negating factors.
- **模型/估值影响：** Base-case 2026E OCF/NI = 0.70 (recovering from Q1 trough); FCF = CNY 505 mn (still positive). Bear case: OCF/NI < 0.50, FCF negative, dividend cut to CNY 0.15/share => DPS yield compresses to ~1.8%, removing the valuation floor and potentially triggering forced selling by income funds. This justifies the bear's lowest-case probability weighting rather than the base case.
- **置信度与下次验证：** medium；H1-2026 cash flow statement: OCF, capex, working-capital components. Receivables and credit-impairment trajectory. Any management commentary on hedging effectiveness and dividend policy. Monthly shareholder-return announcements.

**Q4：Is Liugong's low valuation (PE 10.8x, PB 0.89x) a genuine margin of safety, or a value trap justified by inferior returns and weak cash quality?**
- **为何关键：** If the discount is justified by permanently lower ROE and poor cash conversion, the re-rating argument fails and the stock is dead money even if earnings grow modestly.
- **当前结论：** The valuation discount is partially justified by ROE (7.8%) that is well below better-run peers (Heli 11.6%, Shantui 21.2%) and by weak domestic margins. However, a PB of 0.89x, 5-year PE at the 14.8th percentile, and a price decline that has been entirely multiple-compression (-36.9% PE contraction over 6 months while EPS proxy rose 6.4%) suggest the market has over-shot relative to the actual deterioration. The bull's argument that this is a 'multiple-led derating despite stable EPS' is factually grounded. The PB < 1.0x level does provide some asset-value floor (net asset per share ~CNY 9.3 vs price CNY 8.27), but only if the assets are not impaired by bad receivables or inventory write-downs – precisely the bear's concern. The discount provides a margin of safety for a patient, catalyst-aware buyer, but it is not a standalone reason to buy.
- **采用证据：** Historical price-EPS-PE decomposition: 6M price -32.9%, EPS proxy +6.4%, PE -36.9%. Same-price history: at CNY 8.27, current EPS proxy (CNY 0.765) is 7.9% lower than historical median EPS at similar prices, and PE is 12.4% higher.；Peer comparison: Liugong PE 10.8x vs Heli 12.5x, Shantui 12.9x, Sany ~15x. ROE 7.8% vs Heli 11.6%, Shantui 21.2%.；Market-expectation context: implied TTM earnings CNY 1,558 mn vs FY2025 actual CNY 1,609 mn – market is pricing a slight earnings decline.
- **最强反证：** The bear's point that low PB can reflect genuine asset impairment risk is valid. If CNY 3.5 bn in long-term receivables (primarily dealer floor-plan financing) contains hidden credit losses, the PB floor is softer than it appears. ROIC of only 2.17% (per quality audit) confirms that assets are not earning their cost of capital, justifying a below-book multiple.
- **模型/估值影响：** Base case applies 12x PE on 2027E EPS of CNY 1.12 = CNY 13.44 fair value. This is above the historical median PE of ~16x but deliberately conservative given unresolved cash-quality questions. If the bear's 21% margin and zero domestic growth case materialises, 2027E EPS of ~CNY 0.67 x 8x PE gives CNY 5.36 – a 35% downside. The probability-weighted fair value (50% base, 30% bull, 20% bear) is approximately CNY 13.6 per share (+64% vs CNY 8.27).
- **置信度与下次验证：** medium；H1-2026 asset-quality metrics: receivables ageing, credit impairment charge as % of receivables, inventory composition (finished goods vs WIP). Peer valuation re-rating as the sector catalyst materialises.

**Q5：Why own Liugong over cleaner, higher-ROE peers like Anhui Heli or Shantui if the sector cycle is recovering?**
- **为何关键：** Relative allocation discipline: even if the sector thesis is correct, capital should go to the highest risk/reward expression. If Liugong is merely 'cheap for a reason', a peer substitution is the correct call.
- **当前结论：** Liugong is a higher-beta expression on the sector recovery, not the highest-quality compounder. Heli (PE 12.5x, ROE 11.6%) and Shantui (PE 12.9x, ROE 21.2%) offer cleaner quality-for-value trade-offs, but Liugong offers the largest valuation re-rating potential if three things converge: domestic margin stabilisation, forex normalisation, and the market recognises the structural improvement in overseas earnings. The bull's argument that 'the cheapest name in the sector with the most valuation compression has the most re-rating torque in a recovery' is logically sound for a cyclical-recovery trade. However, if the trade is a long-duration quality compounder, Liugong is inferior. The PM must decide whether this is a catalyst-driven recovery trade (where Liugong's beta helps) or a buy-and-hold allocation (where Heli or Shantui may be better).
- **采用证据：** Peer comparison context: Liugong defensive_score 63.2 vs Zhongji United 69.9, Heli similar score but higher ROE, Shantui ROE 21.2%.；Overseas revenue share: Liugong 47.7% vs Heli ~30%+ (forklifts have different export dynamics). Liugong's overseas margin premium is larger.；Valuation compression: Liugong 6M PE contraction -36.9% vs likely smaller compression for higher-ROE peers (exact peer PE trajectories not supplied, but directionally clear from relative PE levels).
- **最强反证：** Peer context lists Zhongji United (605305), Heli (600761), and Shantui (000680) as potentially better build candidates with higher v4_scores, higher ROE, and higher gross margins. The opportunity cost of tying up capital in a lower-ROE turnaround is real if the sector recovery takes longer than expected.
- **模型/估值影响：** No direct EPS/FCF impact on Liugong's model. Affects position sizing: if the PM wants a pure-quality recovery expression, sizing should be smaller in Liugong and supplemented with Heli or Shantui. If the PM wants maximum beta to the construction-machinery cycle, Liugong is the most undervalued liquid name and justifies a larger weight.
- **置信度与下次验证：** high；Monitor relative price performance and PE re-rating of Liugong vs Heli/Shantui over the next 3–6 months. If Liugong fails to outperform peers during a sector recovery, the 'cheap for a reason' thesis is confirmed and the position should be rotated.

## Ranked Core Theses

### 论点1：The market has over-discounted a cyclical and forex-driven earnings trough, pricing Liugong for permanent stagnation while ignoring a structural overseas profit pool that now contributes nearly half of revenue at a 14pp margin premium.

- **关键问题：** Is the market's implied earnings power (~CNY 1,558 mn, below FY2025 actual) too pessimistic given visible demand signals and overseas profitability?
- **核心证据：** Current PE 10.8x (14.8th percentile). 6M price -32.9% entirely from PE compression (-36.9%) while EPS proxy rose +6.4%. Overseas revenue CNY 15,794 mn (+14.78%), gross margin 29.73% vs domestic 15.75% (FY2025, EV030). Overseas share rising from 45.8% to 47.7% of revenue.
- **最强反证：** Domestic gross margin (15.75% and declining) is barely above breakeven and suggests no domestic pricing power. If overseas growth slows or margin mean-reverts, the profit engine has no domestic backup. The market may be rationally discounting the domestic piece to zero.
- **财务传导：** Overseas revenue growing 15–18% p.a. at 30% gross margin adds CNY 700–900 mn gross profit annually, driving consolidated margin improvement even with stagnant domestic profitability. This flows to EPS growth from CNY 0.80 (FY2025) to CNY 1.12 (2027E base) vs market-implied ~CNY 0.77.
- **市场定价：** Current CNY 8.27 prices a scenario where EPS stays flat at CNY 0.77 or declines. Our base-case 2027E EPS of CNY 1.12 at 12x PE gives CNY 13.44 fair value (+62% upside), suggesting the market has not priced the overseas structural story.
- **证伪条件：** If H1-2026 overseas revenue growth falls below 10% YoY or overseas gross margin contracts below 28%, the structural overseas thesis is weakened. If domestic revenue turns negative and contract liabilities decline sequentially, the demand thesis fails.
- **当前裁决：** partial

### 论点2：The Q1-2026 gross margin and profit compression is dominated by a one-off forex shock, not fundamental manufacturing deterioration; normalisation of the finance-expense line alone can restore CNY 400+ mn in pre-tax profit by 2027E, a tailwind the market is not pricing.

- **关键问题：** Is the margin compression structural (competitive, input-cost driven) or temporary (forex, seasonal mix)?
- **核心证据：** Q1-2026 finance-expense ratio surged to 1.91% (+2.15pp YoY), explaining the majority of the operating margin decline (6.97%, -1.74pp YoY). Derivative liabilities surged to CNY 10.6 mn (EV005), indicating active hedging initiation. KPE04 notes Q2 forex drag substantially reduced. FY2024 had negative finance expense, so the base effect reverses in 2027E.
- **最强反证：** Domestic gross margin 15.75% has been declining for at least two years independent of forex. Even with forex normalisation, the domestic business may be structurally unprofitable at the gross-margin level. Forex can go either direction – a strong CNY would create headwinds, not tailwinds.
- **财务传导：** Finance-expense ratio normalising from 1.9% to 1.0% by 2027E saves ~CNY 350–400 mn in pre-tax profit at CNY 40 bn revenue. This alone adds ~CNY 0.15 to 2027E EPS. Combined with modest gross margin recovery, the bull-bear EPS spread is CNY 0.62 (1.29 vs 0.67).
- **市场定价：** The market's 10.8x PE implies it treats Q1's margin as the new normal rather than a trough. If Q2-2026 data confirms forex normalisation, the re-rating catalyst is immediate and powerful.
- **证伪条件：** If Q2-2026 finance-expense ratio remains above 1.5% and gross margin stays at 21.3% or below, the 'temporary forex shock' thesis is invalidated and structural cost/competition problems must be accepted.
- **当前裁决：** partial

### 论点3：The dividend yield (3.8% trailing) and buyback signal provide a genuine, though not impregnable, downside buffer; the probability of a near-term dividend cut is material (~25–30%) but not high enough to avoid the stock, and the buyback at current prices reinforces management's conviction in undervaluation.

- **关键问题：** Does the dividend provide a real safety net, or is it a trap that will be cut when cash flow weakens further?
- **核心证据：** Dividend Defensive Context rates Liugong 'strong' overall, with dividend coverage 'pass' and trap risk 'low'. FY2025 OCF CNY 1,005 mn covered CNY 662 mn dividend 1.5x. 26-year dividend history, CV 1.06. July-2026 buyback announcement up to CNY 400 mn at max CNY 12.72/share. PB 0.89x provides asset-value reference.
- **最强反证：** FY2025 FCF (OCF – capex) was only CNY 290 mn, covering the dividend just 0.43x on a free-cash basis. Short-term borrowings surged 32% QoQ to CNY 7.53 bn. If 2026E earnings disappoint, management faces a trilemma: invest in working capital, maintain the dividend, or execute the buyback. The buyback is the most dispensable; the dividend may be next.
- **财务传导：** Base-case 2026E FCF CNY 505 mn covers CNY 662 mn dividend 0.76x. Bear case: FCF near zero or negative, dividend halved to CNY 0.15/share => yield compresses to 1.8%, shares may fall 10–15% on re-rating as income funds exit.
- **市场定价：** The 3.8% trailing yield is at the 97.7th percentile historically. The market is either pricing a high probability of a cut or is simply not valuing the dividend at all due to cyclical concerns. Either way, the yield provides a buffer that limits downside to the CNY 7.50–8.00 range unless a cut actually occurs.
- **证伪条件：** Any announcement of a dividend cut, suspension, or a shift to a lower payout ratio; H1-2026 FCF generation materially below CNY 400 mn; buyback not executed or abandoned.
- **当前裁决：** partial

## Company Disaggregation

Liugong's economic engine has four material units. (1) **工程机械行业 – Domestic**: mature core business, 52.3% of 2025 revenue (CNY 17,350 mn, +6.4%), gross margin only 15.75% and declining. This is the cash-consuming, low-return leg. (2) **工程机械行业 – Overseas**: emerging profit engine, 47.7% of 2025 revenue (CNY 15,794 mn, +14.8%), gross margin 29.73% and expanding. ## Debate Verdict

## Debate Verdict: Evidence Reconciliation

The debate pitted a constructive cyclical-recovery thesis (bull) against a cash-flow-sceptic value-trap thesis (bear). Both sides improved the model, and the accepted underwriting plan incorporates key concessions to each.

**What the Bull got right:** The early-cycle demand signals (contract liabilities +43.8%, industry excavator sales +39% in May-2026) are real and cannot be dismissed as pure channel stuffing without evidence. The buyback announcement and derivative hedging initiation provide genuine, if early, signals of management confidence and financial risk management. The structural overseas margin premium (29.7% vs 15.8% domestic) is a durable competitive advantage that the market appears to be undervaluing at 10.8x PE.

**What the Bear got right:** The cash-conversion deterioration (OCF/NI from 1.73 in FY2023 to -0.90 in Q1-2026) is the single most important unresolved risk. The bull's attempt to dismiss this as purely seasonal and forex-driven was only partially successful: the forex component is likely transient, but the three-year trend and the Q1 credit-impairment charge of CNY 94 mn suggest a structural working-capital intensity that the bull underweighted. The bear's insistence that price-hike evidence (KPE03) is unverified sell-side narrative is factually correct – no official filing or announcement confirms the hikes, and until H1-2026 data arrives, margin-recovery assumptions must be tempered.

**Where the bull improved the bear's model:** The bear's zero-growth domestic scenario is inconsistent with the contract-liability build, industry sales data, and the magnitude of the price decline (42% from peak, entirely multiple compression). A market that has derated a stock by 37% on PE while EPS proxy rose 6% is pricing in a recession scenario that current data do not support.

**Where the bear improved the bull's model:** The bull's margin-recovery path was too aggressive (23–24% by 2027E based on unconfirmed price hikes). The bear forced a 1.5pp haircut to the bull's gross margin assumption and introduced explicit OCF/NI and dividend-cut scenarios that were absent from the bull's initial framework. These refinements reduce the base-case fair value from an implied ~CNY 15+ to CNY 13.44, a more defensible target. Additionally, the bear's pressure on capex assumptions led to a more realistic 2% intensity, improving FCF and partially offsetting the OCF downgrade.

**Net effect on the model:**
- Bull's domestic growth assumption: partially accepted (10% not 12%).
- Bull's gross margin assumption: reduced by 1.5–2.0pp.
- Bull's forex normalisation: accepted in direction, moderated in magnitude.
- Bear's cash-flow scepticism: incorporated via conservative OCF/NI (0.70 vs bull's implied 0.90) and explicit 20% bear-case probability with dividend-cut scenario.
- Bear's probability shift: rejected as too aggressive given observable demand signals.
- Capex intensity: reduced to historical normal, improving FCF.

**Overall:** The reconciled model reflects a constructive but appropriately cautious view. The bull's cyclical-recovery thesis survives but with lower conviction and a longer verification path. The bear's warnings have been embedded as explicit risk scenarios rather than accepted as the base case.

## Unresolved Questions and Evidence Gaps

### Gap 1: Price-Hike Confirmation (Critical for Margin Thesis)
- **Issue:** The margin-recovery thesis depends on ASP increases of 2–3% from announced price hikes, but the evidence (KPE03) is a sell-side push classified as `promotional_private_unverified`. - **Required disclosure:** H1-2026 management discussion & analysis – explicit statement on pricing environment, competitive dynamics, and any ASP changes. Alternatively, exchange interaction Q&A or official announcement on price adjustments.

### Gap 2: Channel Inventory and True End-Demand Quality
- **Issue:** Q1-2026 contract liabilities surged 43.8% while short-term borrowings surged 32%. This pattern is consistent with both (a) genuine pre-sales from strong end-demand, and (b) channel stuffing via aggressive dealer financing. No independent dealer inventory data or sell-through data is available.
- **Impact if unresolved:** The domestic demand thesis remains `proxy`-grade evidence. If H1-2026 reveals rising receivables, rising channel inventory, or a sharp slowdown in contract-liability conversion, the bear's 'zero real growth' case becomes the base case.
- **Required disclosure:** H1-2026 MD&A – commentary on dealer inventory levels, channel health, and receivables ageing. Industry association data on dealer inventories. Any independent channel check.

### Gap 3: Segment-Level Profitability (Engineering Machinery Domestic vs Overseas)
- **Issue:** Liugong reports geographic gross margins (domestic 15.75%, overseas 29.73%) but does not split engineering machinery segment gross margin from prestress and rental. The core machinery business may have even lower domestic margins than the geographic split suggests if prestress and rental are included in the domestic figure.
- **Impact if unresolved:** The domestic engineering machinery business may be unprofitable at the gross-profit level once standalone costs are allocated. If domestic machinery gross margin is actually 12–13%, the 15.75% geographic figure is flattered by higher-margin prestress/rental, and the bear's argument of structural domestic unprofitability is reinforced.
- **Required disclosure:** H1-2026 or FY2026 annual report – segment reporting with gross profit by operating segment (engineering machinery, prestress, rental), not just by geography.

### Gap 4: Forex Exposure Breakdown and Hedging Effectiveness
- **Issue:** Q1-2026 finance-expense ratio spiked to 1.91% due to forex losses. - **Required disclosure:** FY2026 annual report – forex sensitivity analysis, hedging policy, and currency composition of overseas revenue. Q2-2026 results as an early proxy for hedging effectiveness.

### Gap 5: Convertible-Bond Dilution Trajectory
- **Issue:** FY2025 diluted EPS (CNY 0.7075) was 11.3% below basic EPS (CNY 0.7978) due to the convertible bond. The conversion pace and the ultimate fully diluted share count are uncertain. Our model uses basic shares (2,037 mn); full conversion could add ~230 mn shares.
